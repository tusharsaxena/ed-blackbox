Briefing

A fast, thin-skinned anti-Xeno skirmisher — three large AX guns and small-pad reach, but heart damage rides only two small Gauss

The Kestrel's three large hardpoints make it a heavy AX gun platform for a small hull, but Guardian Gauss caps at medium size, so the heart-killing battery is limited to its two small mounts. Fast and nimble enough to dodge Interceptor lightning, it survives by evasion rather than tank — thin armour, one military slot and shallow optionals leave it the most fragile hull in the rated AX field. A capable scout-and-Cyclops skirmisher that trades durability for speed and reach.

01

Role & Overview

Anti-Xeno work is a discipline of evasion and precision: Thargoid Interceptors strip modules with lightning, eat hulls with caustic clouds, and reward pilots who can dodge and place clean shots on exposed hearts. The Kestrel Mk II brings genuine speed and small-pad access to that fight, and its three large hardpoints carry a heavy AX multi-cannon battery. But Guardian Gauss — the heart-killer — only fits small and medium mounts, and the Kestrel's only spare mounts are its two smalls, so the precision battery is thin.

It pays for its firepower and speed with survivability. Base armour is just 135, there is a single class-4 military slot, and the optionals are shallow (5·4·3·2·2·2·1) — less Guardian reinforcement room than any rated medium. It cannot soak Thargoid punishment the way a Chieftain or Challenger can; it stays alive by out-flying the lightning. That is why it lands at 60 — a real AX skirmisher, but the frailest and lightest-hitting of the field, best on scouts and lighter Interceptors rather than sustained Titan work.

Where this hull shines

Light, mobile anti-Xeno work: clearing Thargoid scout swarms, skirmishing Cyclops and Basilisk Interceptors, and any AX fight where speed and small-pad reach matter more than a deep tank. Step up to a medium for the heavier Interceptors.

What the Kestrel brings to a Thargoid fight — and what it doesn't:

  • Three large AX mounts. A heavy Enhanced AX Multi-Cannon array for a small hull — strong sustained anti-Thargoid damage and scout clearing.
  • Only two Gauss-capable mounts. Guardian Gauss caps at medium, so the two small mounts carry the heart battery; heart damage is the Kestrel's thinnest axis.
  • Speed to dodge. 271/360 m/s on a nimble hull, pushing past ~450 m/s boost on Dirty Drives — the agility to side-step Interceptor lightning is its whole survival case.
  • One military slot, four utilities. A single class-4 military bay for Guardian reinforcement (rare on a small) plus four utilities for the shield-booster, Xeno Scanner, caustic-sink and heat-sink kit.
The ceiling, stated honestly

Thin 135 armour, one military slot and a 190 t frame mean it hardens far less than any medium AX hull and runs hot feeding three large guns from a class-5 plant. It survives by out-flying the lightning, not by soaking it — misjudge a heart phase and the shallow tank gives out fast. Fly it light, fast, and disciplined on heat.

At ~13.8M Cr the hull carries no rank or permit gate, but the real access cost of an AX fit is the Guardian tech: Guardian Gauss Cannons and the Guardian reinforcement packages are unlocked at Guardian ruins and require Guardian materials, and the Enhanced AX Multi-Cannons come from Tech Brokers. Budget the material runs before the credits.

As an AX platform the Kestrel is a budget, small-pad option — cheaper than most medium AX hulls — justified when you value speed and universal pad access over a deep tank. If you can field a medium pad, a Chieftain buys far more survivability for a similar spend.

Guardian tech is the gate

The credits are modest; the barrier is unlocking Guardian weapons and reinforcement at ruins. Do the Guardian material runs first, then the AX Kestrel comes together cheaply.

3-State Loadout

A small-pad anti-Xeno fit built on the Kestrel's speed: three large Enhanced AX Multi-Cannons for sustained fire, a Guardian Gauss pair on the two small mounts for heart damage, and Guardian reinforcement crammed into one military slot and shallow optionals. Initial is the buy-only bare hull; A-Rated bolts on the Thargoid kit and Guardian reinforcement; Engineered hardens mobility, shields and survival. The AX/Guardian weapons themselves carry no engineering — the gains are in flying and the ship modules.

Kite, place Gauss, harden what you can

The Kestrel survives AX by speed, not tank: dodge Interceptor lightning, line the two small Gauss on exposed hearts and let the three large AX guns hold sustained fire. Hardening is thin — one military slot plus a few Guardian blocks in the optionals — so manage heat hard on a light hull and disengage before the shallow armour matters.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull on stock Lightweight Alloy and stock cores. The AX weapons matter first: arm the three large mounts with gimballed Enhanced AX Multi-Cannons and both small mounts with fixed Guardian Gauss Cannons, then fit a Xeno Scanner so you can read Interceptor hearts.

The bi-weave shield, Guardian reinforcement, decontamination limpet and the caustic/heat-sink launchers wait until the guns are paid off — but even unengineered, three large AX guns clear scout swarms and chip Cyclops hearts, so the buy-only fit is already useful on the lighter Thargoids.

Don't linger. With no reinforcement and a stock shield, the buy-only Kestrel has to disengage the moment a heart phase turns on it.

08

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for a light AX skirmisher:

  • 1Power Distributor → 5A — Gauss drains the WEP capacitor fast; A-rate it first so the pair keeps firing. ~2M Cr
  • 2Power Plant → 5A — Headroom to run three large AX guns, two Gauss and the shield. ~2M Cr
  • 3Thrusters → 5A — Speed is the survival tool; A-rate to keep dodging the lightning. ~1.5M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator → 5C bi-weave — A fast-recharging bi-weave against sustained Thargoid thermal fire. ~1.5M Cr
  • 5Guardian reinforcement — Guardian Module Reinforcement in the military slot, Guardian Hull Reinforcement in the optionals for caustic-resistant armour. ~2M Cr
  • 6FSD → 4A — Reach Thargoid airspace and Maelstroms. ~1M Cr

Fill the four utilities with a shield booster, Xeno Scanner, caustic-sink launcher and heat-sink launcher, and drop a decontamination limpet controller into a mid optional to strip caustic mid-fight.

Feed the guns, keep flying

A-rate the distributor and plant so the Gauss keep firing, then the thrusters so you keep dodging. The Guardian reinforcement is the only real tank a small hull can carry — but speed, not armour, is what keeps this ship alive.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Scout clearing. Three large AX guns shred Thargoid scout swarms — the safest way to learn the discipline and earn AX combat bonds.
  • Cyclops hunting. Two Gauss are enough to crack a Cyclops' hearts with clean flying; ideal first-Interceptor practice.
  • Outpost basing. Small-pad access lets it operate from outpost-only rescue and AX staging stations medium fighters can't reach.
Advanced
  • Anti-Xeno Conflict Zones. Fast repositioning and scout-clearing DPS make it a useful mobile skirmisher, kiting between damage windows.
  • Basilisk skirmishing. Its speed lets it dodge tougher Interceptors, but expect long fights — two small Gauss is slow on the hearts.
  • Maelstrom edge work. Reach distant Maelstroms and run scout/Cyclops work; leave the heavy Interceptors to tankier hulls.
Generic example venues

Any Thargoid-active system with scout activity or a nearby AX Conflict Zone suits it. Rescue megaships and outpost stations in the affected bubble double as small-pad staging the Kestrel can use where mediums cannot dock.

12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Fly it light and fast — speed to dodge the lightning is the Kestrel's only real defence.
  • Manage heat hard: three large AX guns plus two Gauss on a class-5 plant run hot on a small hull; stagger fire and keep a heat sink.
  • Heart damage is the bottleneck — two small Gauss is slow, so pick lighter Interceptors and place every volley.
  • For the heavier Thargoids, step up to an Alliance Chieftain or Krait Mk II — more Gauss and a deeper tank.
Verdict

The Kestrel Mk II is a fast, small-pad anti-Xeno skirmisher: three large AX guns and the speed to dodge Interceptor lightning, let down by only two Gauss-capable mounts and the thinnest tank in the field. It earns its place on scouts and lighter Interceptors, and its universal pad access is unmatched, but a medium is the more capable AX hull whenever you can field one. Fly it light, fast, and disciplined — and don't pick fights the shallow armour can't finish.

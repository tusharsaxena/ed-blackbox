VultureAX Combat

Series Ships Updated 2026-07-01
Briefing

Agile, cheap — and Gauss-less

A famously nimble small-pad hull pressed into anti-Xeno work, and the fit is defined by what it cannot carry. The Vulture has only two large hardpoints and no medium or small mounts, so it cannot mount a single Guardian Gauss Cannon — the role's heart-killer, which caps at medium. Its AX battery is limited to two large AX multi-cannons or a Guardian Shard, and only two guns at that. Elite agility and a rare class-5 military slot keep it viable as a cheap scout-clearer, but a power-starved plant and the thinnest heart-DPS in the field pin it near the bottom of the AX ladder.

01

Role & Overview

Anti-Xeno combat is a discipline of evasion and precision: Thargoid Interceptors strip modules with lightning, eat hulls with caustic clouds, and reward pilots who can dodge and place clean shots on exposed hearts. Guardian Gauss Cannons are the heart-killers — but they fit only small and medium mounts, and the Vulture has neither. Its two hardpoints are both Large, so the AX battery is limited to two large AX multi-cannons or a Guardian Shard Cannon. Two guns, none of them Gauss-capable, is the thinnest heart-damage layout in the rated field.

What the Vulture keeps is its defining strength — elite agility — and one genuine bright spot for a small hull: a class-5 military slot, big enough for a size-5 Guardian reinforcement block that most rated mediums can't match. But the optionals are shallow (5·4·2·1·1·1·1), only four utility mounts carry the whole support kit, and the class-4 power plant cannot comfortably run two large guns plus the shield. That is why it lands at 44 — a cheap, agile hull that clears scouts and skirmishes light Interceptors, but cannot mount the weapon the role is built around.

Where this hull shines

Budget scout-clearing and light-Interceptor skirmishing: cheap entry into anti-Xeno work, small-pad reach to any outpost, and enough agility to dodge the lightning. Step up to an agile AX medium the moment you want real heart damage.

What decides the Vulture's Thargoid fight:

  • No Gauss mount. Two Large hardpoints and nothing smaller — Guardian Gauss caps at medium, so the hull cannot carry the role's key precision weapon at all. It fights with two large AX multi-cannons or a Guardian Shard instead.
  • Elite agility. Class-leading turn rate lets it dodge Interceptor lightning and hold guns on an exposed heart — its whole survival case, though a 340 m/s boost is modest for opening distance from a swarm.
  • A rare class-5 military slot. Unusual on a small hull — room for a size-5 Guardian reinforcement block, the single biggest survival gain the Vulture can make.
  • Cheap and small-pad. Under 5M credits with no rank gate and outpost-anywhere access — the lowest-cost way into AX work.
The ceiling, stated honestly

Two guns, no Gauss, and a class-4 power plant that struggles to run them alongside the shield mean heart damage is slow and power is tight. Four utility mounts and shallow optionals cap the caustic/support kit, and the hull tanks lightning far worse than the military-slotted mediums. The Vulture is a cheap skirmisher and scout-clearer — not a Titan boat.

At ~4.7M Cr with no rank or permit gate, the Vulture is the cheapest hull that can meaningfully enter anti-Xeno work. The AX and Guardian weapons and the Guardian reinforcement modules require the Guardian tech-broker unlocks — a prerequisite for any AX ship, not specific to this one.

The value case is real but narrow: a few million credits and low rebuy make it a fearless scout-clearer. It will not carry you into sustained Interceptor or Titan fights — that is a medium's job.

Cheapest way in

Under 5M credits for a hull that can clear Thargoid scouts and skirmish light Interceptors. The ceiling is low, but so is the buy-in — a first AX hull for pilots who already fly a Vulture.

Dodge and manage power — it can't out-tank or out-Gauss anything

The Vulture survives AX by out-flying the lightning, not soaking it. Dodge the Interceptor's attacks, clear caustic with the sink launcher and a heat-sink overheat, repair stripped modules with the AFMU, and watch the class-4 plant — two large AX guns plus the shield leave almost no power margin. With no Gauss mount and only two guns, heart damage is slow; this is a scout-clearing and light-Interceptor hull, not a Titan boat.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull (~4.7M Cr); its stock Lightweight Alloy bulkheads stay for now — Military Grade is an A-rated upgrade. Fit a 5E shield generator, one 0E shield booster, a basic AFMU for module repair, and a 5E military-slot hull reinforcement.

Arm both large mounts buy-only with basic AX multi-cannons — they need no Guardian unlock and are enough to learn Thargoid fights. Set power priorities so weapons and shields don't brown out when you deploy hardpoints and boost.

08

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for the AX Vulture — power first, always:

  • 1Power Plant → A — The chronic bottleneck; A-rate and Overcharge it before anything else so two large AX guns and the shield stay lit. ~1.5M Cr
  • 2Power Distributor → A — The Shard and AX multi-cannon drain the WEP capacitor fast. ~1.2M Cr
  • 3Thrusters → A — Maximise the agility that dodges Interceptor lightning. ~1.5M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator → bi-weave — Fast regen for a skirmisher that re-engages between passes. ~1M Cr
  • 5Guardian Hull Reinforcement → class-5 military slot — The single biggest survival block, with built-in caustic resistance. Guardian unlock
  • 6FSD → A — Reach Thargoid airspace and rescue megaships. ~1M Cr
Plant before everything

On the Vulture, power comes first — an Overcharged class-4 plant is the difference between two large AX guns that fire continuously and a ship that stalls mid-fight. The Guardian reinforcement and Thargoid kit come once the guns run.

09

Engineering Plan

The house AX pattern with a hard focus on power and mobility; the AX and Guardian weapons themselves are not engineerable. Pin blueprints for remote G1→G5 application; visit in person for experimentals.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Thargoid scout clearing. The Vulture's agility and two AX multi-cannons make short work of scout swarms — the ideal first AX task.
  • Guardian unlocks. Before any serious AX work, unlock the Guardian weapons and reinforcement at the Guardian ruins and tech broker.
  • Notice-board AX massacre. Cheap rebuy lets you take AX kill missions in scout-heavy systems without fear.
Advanced
  • Light Interceptor skirmishing. Dodge lightning, strip one heart at a time with the Shard on close passes — slow, but survivable in skilled hands.
  • Wing support. Fly as a mobile scout-clearer and caustic-decoy alongside heavier Gauss boats that do the heart damage.
  • Know when to step up. For Basilisk, Medusa and Titan work, move to an agile AX medium — the Vulture cannot carry the Gauss those fights demand.
Generic example systems

Any system in or near a Maelstrom's Thargoid-controlled space suits scout clearing; nearby rescue megaships sell AX weapons and repair modules. Keep an outpost with a small pad in reach for turn-ins.

12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • No Gauss, ever — the hull has no medium or small mount, so heart damage rides two large AX guns and is slow by design.
  • Power is the skill — Overcharge the plant and set power priorities before you undock, or the guns cut out mid-pass.
  • Clear caustic with the sink launcher and a heat-sink overheat; there is no cargo room for a decontamination limpet.
  • Use the class-5 military slot for a size-5 Guardian reinforcement — the biggest single survival gain the hull can make.
Verdict

The Vulture is the budget on-ramp to anti-Xeno work: cheap, agile and small-pad, but crippled for the role by two large hardpoints that cannot mount a single Guardian Gauss Cannon. It clears scouts and skirmishes light Interceptors well enough to justify its 44, and no rated AX hull costs less — but the moment you want real heart damage, an agile AX medium is the answer.

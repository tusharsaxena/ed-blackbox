01

Role & Overview

The Alliance Challenger is the brawler of the Alliance family. It shares the Chieftain's chassis and its three military slots, but re-weights the design toward durability: seven weapon mounts (one large, three medium, three small), the heaviest base shields of the trio, and a tougher hull — at the cost of the Chieftain's signature agility.

That makes it a different kind of fighter. Where the Chieftain dances, the Challenger anchors: it plants itself in a conflict zone and grinds, soaking enormous punishment across its shields, hull and three reinforcement slots while seven guns chew through the opposition. For attrition fights — long CZ sessions, tough massacre stacks, AX engagements — it's the toughest medium short of a large warship.

Where this hull shinesSustained attrition combat: long conflict-zone sessions, heavy pirate-massacre stacks, and AX work where standing in the fight and out-lasting the enemy matters more than out-turning them.

Four things make the Challenger the trio's brawler:

  • Seven weapon mounts. One large, three medium and three small — the most total hardpoints of the Alliance trio, enough for a heavy main battery plus a full utility-weapon spread.
  • Best shields of the trio. The highest base shield of the three Alliance hulls, layered over the heaviest armour — double-tough.
  • Three military slots. Like its siblings, dedicated reinforcement bays that tank the hull without eating shield or cargo room.
  • Deep optionals. Two class-6 optionals allow a big shield plus cell banks — sustain to match the durability.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIt gives up the Chieftain's agility — it's slower and turns wider, so it can't hold guns on a nimble target the way the Chieftain can, and it's easier to flank. Its DPS ceiling is solid but not the highest. The Challenger's argument is pure staying power; if a fight rewards dancing rather than grinding, the Chieftain is the better Alliance pick.

At ~29.6M Cr the Challenger costs more than the Chieftain but remains mid-priced for its durability, with no rank or permit gate. The extra credits buy more hardpoints, better shields and a tougher hull — everything you're paying for goes into staying power.

Its deep optionals and three military slots mean a full tank-and-sustain fit adds up, but the result is a medium that survives fights that would destroy lesser ships several times over.

Toughness per creditIf survivability is your priority, the Challenger offers more of it per credit than any other medium — a fortress that docks on a medium pad and needs no rank.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fit the largest shield the class-6 bay allows, then fill the military slots with hull reinforcement.

Arm the large mount with a gimballed multi-cannon and spread beams/multi-cannons across the mediums and smalls.

Even unengineered it's a fortress — ready for CZ attrition immediately.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Conflict zones. Plant the Challenger in a CZ and grind combat bonds while it soaks everything thrown at it.
  • Hazardous RES. Its durability lets it work Haz RES indefinitely, clearing high-value bounties at leisure.
  • Pirate massacre stacks. Stack massacre missions and clear them in long, safe sessions.
Advanced
  • High-intensity CZ attrition. Engineered, it dominates the toughest conflict zones by simply refusing to die.
  • Thargoid AX combat. Its toughness suits AX engagements, though the more agile Chieftain is often preferred for dodging. (A dedicated AX manual covers this.)
  • Anchor in wing combat. It makes an ideal tank in a wing, holding aggro while wingmates deal damage.
Generic example systemsAny Haz RES or CZ suits it; the higher the intensity, the more its durability pays off. Around your Diaguandri / Ray Gateway base, CZ and massacre payouts double as Imperial-standing progress.
12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Build it to tank on both layers — strong shield plus three military-slot reinforcements — to exploit its defining strength.
  • Accept the lower agility; play it as an anchor that holds position and grinds, not a dancer.
  • Its seven mounts suit a mix of hull-damage multi-cannons and shield-stripping beams — cover both damage types.
  • If you find yourself wanting to dodge and weave, the Chieftain is the better Alliance hull for that style.
VerdictThe Alliance Challenger is the toughest stand-and-fight medium you can buy without a rank grind. It trades the Chieftain's agility for more guns, better shields and a heavier hull, becoming a fortress that anchors a conflict zone and out-lasts everything its size. For attrition combat, it's the Alliance hull to fly.

01

Role & Overview

The Mandalay is best known as the SCO-era benchmark explorer, but its specification reads like a generalist's: four medium hardpoints, ten optional internals, four utilities and a light, fast, agile frame. That combination lets one hull explore the black, fight in a RES, haul cargo and run mission boards — all while travelling further and faster than almost anything in its class.

Where it differs from the classic medium multiroles is emphasis. The Python and Krait are heavier, tougher and built to fight first; the Mandalay is light, quick and built to travel first. As an all-rounder it trades a little durability for far more range and speed — an appealing balance for commanders who roam widely and fight occasionally.

Where this hull shinesWide-ranging mixed play: explore and exobio one session, bounty hunt or run missions the next, haul a load of trade goods across the bubble after that — all from one fast, far-reaching hull.

Four things make the Mandalay a capable, distinctive all-rounder:

  • Four medium hardpoints. More medium guns than most generalists — enough firepower to make the combat side of multirole work genuine, not token.
  • Ten optional internals. Exceptional flexibility: cargo, cabins, mining kit or full expedition modules, with slots left for shields and reinforcement.
  • Class-leading range and SCO speed. It reaches and crosses systems faster than any rival multirole — the whole bubble, and beyond, is within easy reach.
  • Light and agile. High speed and nimble handling make it forgiving in a fight and quick on every errand.
The ceiling, stated honestlyA Python or Krait Mk II is tougher, better shielded and hits harder — the Mandalay's lighter frame and smaller core power mean it's a softer brawler and a lighter hauler than the heavyweight mediums. Its multirole case rests on doing every job quickly and at extreme range rather than on raw toughness or capacity.

The hull is around 16.5M Cr for Odyssey owners (non-Odyssey owners unlock it with ARX first). There is no rank or permit gate. A working multirole fit lands near 28M Cr; a fully engineered version sits around 45M+ depending on how combat-focused you make it.

Because it re-roles by swapping optionals, the Mandalay rewards engineering the cores once — especially the FSD — then changing the bay per job. Its low mass means even a loaded fit keeps usable range.

One hull, many jobsEngineer the FSD, thrusters, plant and distributor once; then swap optional modules to switch between exploration, cargo, combat and missions. The Mandalay keeps more range than any rival when you load it for non-exploration work.

ARX Pre-Built Option

There is no dedicated ARX pre-built package built around the Mandalay multirole; buy and outfit it normally through a shipyard (Odyssey required, or unlock via ARX).

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fit a shield, a hull reinforcement and a couple of cargo racks — it's ready for mixed work immediately.

Arm the four mediums with gimballed multi-cannons; add small pulse lasers for shield-stripping.

Keep an SRV bay and a basic scanner aboard so you can opportunistically explore and exobio between other jobs.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Explore-and-earn loops. Run exobiology and first-discovery sweeps, then haul the data and any salvage home and switch to mission work — one hull, two careers.
  • Mixed mission boards. Its speed and four mediums handle courier, data and bounty missions with ease.
  • RES bounty hunting. Four medium hardpoints clear low and medium RES targets; the light frame keeps you mobile.
Advanced
  • Far-flung operations. Reach distant systems other multiroles can't, then fight, haul or scan once you arrive — the Mandalay's range turns the whole galaxy into a worksite.
  • Re-role on demand. Saved loadouts let it flip between exploration, trade and combat as opportunities shift.
  • Powerplay reach. Fast and far-reaching for fortification and merit runs across widely spaced systems.
Generic example systemsAny region works, but the Mandalay rewards routes other ships find too long. From your Diaguandri / Ray Gateway base it can strike out to distant systems for exploration or missions and return faster than a heavier multirole could.
12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Lean into the range: take the jobs and routes that punish slower, shorter-legged ships.
  • Keep an SRV bay and scanners aboard permanently — the transparent floor makes opportunistic exobiology effortless.
  • Accept the softer hull; play it as a fast skirmisher and traveller, not a stand-and-tank brawler.
  • Lightweight life support and sensors recover even more of its class-leading range.
VerdictThe Mandalay is the all-rounder for commanders who roam. It gives up some toughness and cargo to the heavyweight mediums, but in return it travels further and faster than anything in its class while still mounting four mediums and ten optionals. If your idea of multipurpose includes crossing the galaxy between jobs, nothing does it better.

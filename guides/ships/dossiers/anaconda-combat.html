01

Role & Overview

The Anaconda is Faulcon DeLacy's flagship and the game's archetypal do-everything capital ship. In combat it fields eight hardpoints — a huge mount, three large, two medium and two small — behind a 945 base armour hull, eight utility mounts, a single military slot, twelve optional internals and a ship-launched-fighter bay. It is, in every sense, a battleship.

Crucially, it has no rank gate — unlike the Federal Corvette and Imperial Cutter, anyone with the credits can buy one. That makes it the accessible large warship: not quite as combat-focused or agile as the rank-gated apex pair, but more flexible than either and open to everyone. It fights, hauls, explores and runs missions at a scale only large ships reach.

Where this hull shines

Heavy combat without a rank grind: high-intensity conflict zones, Hazardous RES, pirate massacres, AX work and wing fights — plus the flexibility to re-role the same hull to hauling or exploration at scale.

Four things make the Anaconda a formidable combat capital ship:

  • Eight hardpoints with a huge mount. A capital-grade battery — huge plus three large plus four smaller mounts — that out-guns any medium and rivals the dedicated large warships.
  • Battleship armour and vast internals. A 945 armour base, twelve optionals and a military slot let it tank to extraordinary levels while still carrying everything it needs.
  • Eight utility mounts and an SLF bay. A huge defensive utility suite plus a launched fighter make it a force unto itself.
  • No rank gate. Unlike the Corvette and Cutter, it needs only credits — the most accessible capital warship in the game.
The ceiling, stated honestly

It's slow and ponderous — the worst agility of the large combat ships — so nimble enemies can work its blind spots, and it's costly to engineer fully. The Federal Corvette out-guns and out-turns it, and the Imperial Cutter out-shields it. The Anaconda's edge is flexibility and accessibility, not peak combat focus; it's the jack-of-all-trades capital, master of none.

At ~142M Cr the Anaconda is a major investment, but it carries no rank or permit gate — the most accessible capital warship. A full combat fit with engineered guns, shields and reinforcement pushes the all-in figure past 220M Cr, comparable to the rank-gated apex ships.

Because it re-roles so completely — battleship one week, deep-space explorer or bulk hauler the next — a single engineered Anaconda can replace several specialist ships, spreading its considerable cost across many roles.

The credits-only capital

Unlike the Corvette (Federal Rear Admiral) and Cutter (Imperial Duke), the Anaconda needs only money. If you want a battleship without a navy grind, this is it.

ARX Pre-Built Option

There is no combat-specific ARX pre-built built around the Anaconda; buy and outfit it normally through a shipyard.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fit the largest shield a class-7 bay allows plus hull reinforcement — even basic, it's a tank.

Arm the huge and large mounts with gimballed multi-cannons; add beams for shield-stripping.

Add the fighter bay early; an SLF multiplies a capital ship's already considerable presence.

08

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for a combat capital ship:

  • 1Power Distributor → A — Eight guns plus boost demand the class-8 distributor first. ~20M Cr
  • 2Power Plant → A — Headroom for guns, a big shield, cells, SLF and eight utilities. ~25M Cr
  • 3Thrusters → A — Recover what agility a 400t-mass capital can manage. ~18M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator → bi-weave / prismatic — A capital-scale shield is the first tank layer. ~15M Cr
  • 5FSD → A — Move a heavy battleship between fights and turn-ins. ~15M Cr
Power for a battleship

Eight hardpoints, a fighter and eight utilities draw enormous power — A-rate the class-8 distributor and plant before anything else or the build can't run at load.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • High-intensity conflict zones. A capital battery and battleship tank let the Anaconda dominate the toughest CZs for combat bonds.
  • Hazardous RES. It clears the highest-value bounties at leisure, soaking everything in return.
  • Pirate massacre stacks. Stack missions and clear them from a near-immortal platform.
Advanced
  • Thargoid AX combat. Its hardpoint count, utilities and tank make it a strong AX capital with the right weapons and guardian modules.
  • Wing anchor and capital presence. As a battleship with an SLF and eight utilities it anchors any wing and area-denies the field.
  • Re-role at scale. Swap the loadout to make the same hull a long-range explorer or a bulk hauler — capital-scale flexibility.
Generic example systems

Any high-intensity CZ or Haz RES suits it; for AX, head to Thargoid-active systems. As a credits-only capital it's a fine combat flagship to fly while grinding Imperial or Federal rank toward a Cutter or Corvette.

12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Embrace the scale: tank on shields and hull at once, carry a fighter, and field a full eight-utility booster suite — few ships can do all three.
  • Accept the poor agility; play it as an immovable battleship that out-guns and out-lasts, not a dancer.
  • It's the credits-only capital — the ideal large warship to own while grinding the ranks for a Corvette or Cutter.
  • Fully engineering it is a major material spend; prioritise distributor, plant, shield and the huge mount first.
Verdict

The Anaconda is the do-everything battleship: eight hardpoints with a huge mount, vast internals, a fighter bay and battleship armour, all with no rank gate. The Corvette and Cutter beat it for pure combat focus, but neither matches its flexibility or accessibility. For a credits-only capital warship that also hauls and explores at scale, the Anaconda is in a class of its own.

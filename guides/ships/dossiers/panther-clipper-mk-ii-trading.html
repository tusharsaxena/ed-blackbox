01

Role & Overview

The Panther Clipper Mk II is Lakon's dedicated super-freighter and the new king of bulk hauling. Thirteen optional internals — two of them class 8 — give it a maximum cargo capacity north of a thousand tonnes, more than any other ship in the game, including the venerable Type-9 and the Imperial Cutter. It is, simply, the most cargo you can move in one hull.

Crucially, it's not just a bigger box. Six utility mounts and a tough, purpose-built frame make it far more defensible than the Type-9, so it can run high-value or hostile routes carrying a fortune without being easy prey. It's slow and large-pad-bound, and it costs a fortune to buy — but for a commander whose business is moving the maximum tonnage per trip, the Panther is the definitive answer.

Where this hull shinesMaximum-tonnage bulk trading: high-volume commodity runs, Community Goal hauling, fleet-carrier resupply, and any operation where moving the most cargo per trip — defensibly — is the entire point.

Four things make the Panther the ultimate freighter:

  • The largest cargo hold in the game. Over 1,000 tonnes — more than the Type-9 or Cutter — the most you can move in a single hull.
  • Six utility mounts. Room for a real shield-booster and defence suite, so it can run hostile routes carrying a fortune.
  • A tough, purpose-built frame. Far more defensible than the flimsy Type-9 — it survives interdictions a lesser hauler wouldn't.
  • No rank gate. Unlike the Cutter, anyone with the credits can fly one — the accessible super-hauler.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIt's enormous, slow and large-pad-bound, so it can't use outposts and handles like the freighter it is, and at ~287M Cr it's one of the most expensive ships in the game to buy. Its jump range laden is modest. The Panther's case is pure maximum-tonnage hauling; for flexibility, pad access or value, a medium trader or the cheaper Type-9 makes more sense.

At ~287M Cr the Panther is among the most expensive ships in the game, though it needs no rank or permit. A trade fit needs little exotic engineering — mostly thrusters, FSD and a defensive shield — so the bulk of the cost is the hull and cargo racks.

It pays for itself only at scale: if your income is bulk commodity hauling or Community Goal deliveries, the Panther's extra hundreds of tonnes per trip compound fast. For occasional trading, a cheaper Type-9 or a medium trader is the wiser buy.

Pays off at scaleThe Panther's edge is hundreds of extra tonnes per trip — worth it only if bulk hauling is your business. For casual trading, the cheaper Type-9 carries nearly as much.

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fill the optionals with the largest cargo racks — capacity is the point.

Keep one class-6 slot for a bi-weave shield plus boosters on the utilities for defensible hauling.

A-rate thrusters and FSD so the laden ship still moves and jumps reasonably.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Bulk commodity runs. Move the maximum tonnage of high-value goods per trip between profitable markets.
  • Community Goal hauling. Its capacity tops CG delivery leaderboards faster than anything else.
  • Fleet-carrier resupply. Fill a carrier's stores in the fewest possible trips.
Advanced
  • Defended high-value routes. Its shields and utilities let it carry a fortune through hostile space others daren't.
  • Colonisation and megaship supply. Bulk-deliver construction materials at scale.
  • Rare-goods circuits. Carry the maximum allocation of every rare on a long loop.
Generic example routesAny high-volume, high-profit commodity loop suits it. From your Diaguandri / Ray Gateway base, the Panther turns bulk-hauling income into a serious business — ideally paired with a fleet carrier.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Cargo is the point — fill the internals, but keep a bi-weave and boosters for valuable routes; you have the slots.
  • A-rate and engineer thrusters and FSD so the laden ship still moves and jumps.
  • Mind the large pad — plan around orbital stations, planetary ports or a carrier.
  • It pays off only at scale; for casual trading, the cheaper Type-9 carries nearly as much.
VerdictThe Panther Clipper Mk II is the ultimate freighter: over a thousand tonnes of cargo — the most in the game — behind a defensible frame and six utilities, with no rank gate. It's vast, slow and hugely expensive, but for the single business of moving the maximum tonnage per trip safely, nothing else comes close. The dedicated trade flagship.

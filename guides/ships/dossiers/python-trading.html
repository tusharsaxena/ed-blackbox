01

Role & Overview

The Python is the trader that doesn't need an escort. It carries ~294 tonnes on a medium pad — a solid load — but its real distinction is that the same hull is a tough, three-large-hardpoint warship. Where a Type-9 or Type-8 must run from pirates, a combat-fitted-then-cargo-loaded Python can simply turn and destroy them. It defends its own cargo.

It carries less than the dedicated Type-8 and costs more, so for pure tonnage it isn't the pick. But its blend of solid cargo, medium-pad flexibility, strong shields and the option to fight back makes it the safest active hauler — and because it re-roles to combat, mining or exploration, one Python can be a trader that becomes a warship the moment a pirate makes a mistake. A classic, versatile choice.

Where this hull shinesDefensible, versatile trading: high-value runs through pirate space where fighting back beats fleeing, mixed trade-and-combat play, and a single hull that hauls one day and fights or mines the next.

Four things make the Python a fine defensible trader:

  • It defends its own cargo. Three large hardpoints and strong shields mean it can fight off pirates a dedicated freighter must flee.
  • Solid cargo on a medium pad. ~294 tonnes landing at most stations — a good load with flexible docking.
  • Strong shields and deep internals. It tanks interdiction comfortably and has room for a big hold plus defences.
  • Total versatility. The same hull trades, fights, mines or explores — one ship, many jobs.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIt carries far less than the dedicated freighters — the medium Type-8 hauls 100+ tonnes more for less money — and it's slow. For pure cargo-per-credit it's beaten. The Python's case is defensibility and versatility: it's the trader you pick when you'd rather fight pirates than outrun them, or want one hull for everything.

At ~55.3M Cr the Python is pricier than the dedicated medium freighters, with no rank gate. A defensible trade fit — cargo plus shields plus a few engineered guns — brings the all-in figure to around 90M Cr.

Its versatility offsets the cost: the same engineered Python trades, fights, mines or explores, so one hull replaces several. For pure hauling, the cheaper Type-8 is the value pick; for a do-everything ship that also hauls well and defends itself, the Python justifies its price.

Versatility is the valueEngineer one Python and it's a trader, a warship and a miner by swapping modules — a hauler that can fight, for the price of its flexibility.

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fill most optionals with cargo, keeping one class-6 slot for a bi-weave shield.

Arm the three large mounts with gimballed multi-cannons so you can fight off pirates.

A-rate thrusters, plant and distributor so the laden ship moves and sustains its guns.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • High-value runs through pirate space. Fight off interdiction instead of fleeing — the Python's signature.
  • Mixed trade-and-combat. Haul cargo and clear bounties or missions in the same hull.
  • Medium-pad commodity loops. A solid defended load to most stations.
Advanced
  • Dangerous-route hauling. Where a Type-9 would be robbed, the Python turns and wins.
  • Re-role on demand. Swap modules to make the trader a warship, miner or explorer.
  • Powerplay logistics under threat. Move cargo through contested space and defend it.
Generic example routesAny valuable loop, especially through risky space, suits it. From your Diaguandri / Ray Gateway base it hauls defended cargo and re-roles to combat or mining as needed — one hull, many jobs.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Keep the guns — the Python's edge over pure freighters is fighting back; don't strip them for a little more cargo.
  • Power plant and distributor must sustain guns while laden — A-rate them.
  • For pure tonnage, the cheaper Type-8 carries more; choose the Python for defensibility and versatility.
  • It has combat, mining and multipurpose manuals too — the same hull does it all.
VerdictThe Python is the defensible medium hauler: ~294 tonnes on a medium pad, behind strong shields and three large guns that let it destroy the pirates other traders must flee. The Type-8 out-hauls it for less, but no medium trader defends its cargo better or re-roles so completely. The hauler that fights back, and a true do-everything classic.

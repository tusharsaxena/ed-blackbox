01

Role & Overview

The Asp Explorer is a deep-space hull first and a passenger ship second — but the same traits that make it a great explorer make it a serviceable tourer. Its long FSD reach takes sightseeing boards to scattered scenic beacons in fewer hops than a small liner, it lands on a medium pad (and outposts) the dedicated large liners can't use, and it's cheap enough that most commanders already have one in the hangar. For a first passenger ship, or a part-time tourer that doubles as your explorer, it earns its keep.

The limits are real, though. The Asp has no liner comfort bonus, so passengers pay standard rates rather than the premium a Dolphin, Orca or Beluga commands; and only its two largest internals (class 6 and class 5) can hold first or luxury cabins, so it can't stack the high-value VIP berths that make passenger work lucrative. It carries a modest economy board well and far, but it isn't a money-printer per seat. The reach-and-access budget pick, not the luxury earner.

Where this hull shines

Cheap economy sightseeing runs to scattered or distant beacons; outpost-and-medium-pad tourist boards the large liners can't reach; and any explorer-owner who wants to fly the occasional passenger contract without buying a second hull.

Four things shape the Asp as a passenger hull — two strengths, two ceilings:

  • Long reach for a medium. The explorer-grade FSD takes sightseeing boards to scattered, distant beacons most small liners can't service in one hop.
  • Medium-pad and outpost access. Lands where the big liners can't — opens up tourist boards routed through smaller stations.
  • No comfort bonus. A Lakon hull, not a luxury liner — passengers pay standard rates, so per-seat earnings trail every dedicated tourer.
  • Limited premium berths. Only the class 6 and class 5 slots take first/luxury cabins, so it can't stack the high-value VIP fares that make passenger work pay.
The ceiling, stated honestly

The Asp isn't really a liner — it's an explorer running tourist boards. With no comfort bonus and only two slots that fit premium cabins, a Dolphin earns far more per seat on a small pad, and the dedicated large liners out-berth and out-earn it many times over. Its case is cheap reach and medium-pad access, not luxury pay.

At ~6.1M Cr the hull is among the cheapest credible passenger ships, with no permit, rank or engineer gate and no expansion requirement. A sensible passenger fit — a premium cabin in the top slot, economy below, a bi-weave shield and a long-range FSD — runs to around ~30M Cr all-in. The rebuy is light at roughly ~1.5M Cr, the lowest of any ship in this dossier set.

The real saving is double duty: the cabins swap out for an exploration suite at a station, so the same engineered hull flies tourists this week and the black the next. For a commander who already owns an Asp explorer, adding a cabin set is the cheapest possible entry into passenger work.

Cheapest entry, smallest premium

Around ~30M Cr all-in — no gates, a featherweight rebuy, and the cabins swap for an explorer suite. You pay least to start; you also earn least per seat, with no comfort bonus to lean on.

Premium up top, economy below

Put your one high-value first/luxury cabin in the class-6 slot — it's where the per-seat money lives — and fill the rest with economy. 07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fit a starter cabin suite — a 6E Economy Passenger Cabin in the class-6 slot and two 3E Economy cabins below it, with a 5D Shield Generator in the class-5 slot and two 0E Shield Boosters for safety. A 1I Detailed Surface Scanner sits in the size-1 bay.

Fit the 5A SCO Frame Shift Drive first — it's the one core you buy A-rated from the start; range and in-system speed are what let the Asp take scattered sightseeing boards.

Leave the two spare utility mounts and the smaller optional slots empty for now — they fill out at A-rating — and you have a cheap, expedition-ready tourer for well under 20M Cr all-in.

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for a budget passenger hull:

  • 1FSD → 5A (SCO) — The defining stat; reach takes the distant boards. ~5M Cr
  • 2Premium cabin (class 6) — One first/luxury cabin up top is where the per-seat money is. varies
  • 3Bi-Weave shield (class 5) — Passenger safety on threatened routes. ~3M Cr
  • 4Thrusters → A — Mobility for the loaded hull. ~2M Cr
  • 5Power Plant → 5A & Distributor → 4A — Power and pips for cabins, shield and boost. ~4M Cr
  • 6Shield boosters & board fill-out — A-rate both boosters to 0A, slot a 1H Guardian FSD Booster in the size-1 bay, and pack the rest with a 3D Business cabin and economy berths (plus heat-sink and point-defence utilities). varies
Range first, then the cabin mix

The Asp earns on reach and price, not a comfort bonus — A-rate the FSD before anything, then fit the one premium cabin it can carry and pack economy below. Add a Guardian FSD booster to stretch the range further.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Economy sightseeing tours. Cheap tourist boards to scenic beacons — the bread-and-butter the Asp does well.
  • Medium-pad & outpost boards. Tourist contracts routed through smaller stations the large liners can't dock at.
  • Part-time passenger runs. Slot a cabin set into your explorer Asp when a lucrative board appears.
Advanced
  • Distant-beacon tours. Far-flung sightseeing destinations where the Asp's reach beats a small liner's range.
  • VIP single-cabin runs. Carry one high-value first/luxury passenger up top while economy fills the rest.
  • Dual-purpose cruising. Swap cabins for an explorer suite mid-trip as the week demands.
Generic example routes

Tourist-beacon boards that wander to scattered or distant systems suit it best, where reach and medium-pad access matter more than per-seat luxury. From your home base, the Asp takes the cheap, far-flung tours a small liner can't reach.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • A-rate and engineer the FSD first — reach is what the Asp brings to passenger work; add a Guardian booster.
  • There's no liner comfort bonus — it earns less per seat than a Dolphin, Orca or Lynx; lean on cheap reach and medium-pad access instead.
  • Only the class 6 and class 5 slots take first/luxury cabins — put your one premium cabin up top and pack economy below.
  • Keep a real shield on threatened routes; passenger boards can spawn pirate interdictions.
  • Swap the cabins for an explorer suite and the same hull changes roles — see the Asp Explorer Exploration and Multipurpose dossiers.
Verdict

The Asp Explorer is the budget passenger pick: cheapest entry, a featherweight rebuy, long reach and a medium pad that takes boards the large liners can't. 60/100 — held back, fairly, by having no liner comfort bonus and only two slots that fit premium cabins, so it earns less per seat than any dedicated tourer. Not the earner; the one most commanders already own and can fly far for next to nothing.

01

Role & Overview

The Imperial Cutter is Gutamaya's flagship and the Empire's answer to the Federal Corvette. Where the Corvette wins on guns and agility, the Cutter wins on protection: it carries the strongest shields in the game by a wide margin — over 700 MJ at base, well past 2,500 engineered — over a 720 armour hull, two military slots and vast internals. It is, quite simply, the hardest ship to destroy.

It backs that tank with real teeth: a huge hardpoint, two large and four medium mounts give it a heavy seven-gun battery, and for a 1,100-tonne ship it's surprisingly fast in a straight line. Its weakness is agility — it turns like the moon it resembles — so it fights as an immovable shield fortress rather than a duellist. Like the Corvette, it sits at the top of a navy: Imperial Duke.

Where this hull shines

Attrition combat and survival: the longest, hardest conflict zones, Thargoid AX, wing tanking and any fight where being impossible to kill matters more than turning quickly. The shield-tank endgame.

Four things make the Cutter the shield-tank apex:

  • The strongest shields in the game. Over 700 MJ at base and well past 2,500 engineered with a prismatic generator — nothing else comes close to its shield tank.
  • A huge hardpoint plus six mounts. A heavy seven-gun battery headlined by a huge mount — formidable firepower behind the unmatched shields.
  • Two military slots and vast internals. Dedicated reinforcement plus huge optionals layer a hull tank under the shields and feed massive cell banks.
  • Fast for a capital. A 323 m/s boost is brisk for a 1,100-tonne ship — it can close, reposition and even chase in a straight line.
The ceiling, stated honestly

Its agility is dreadful — it cannot turn with anything, so nimble enemies sit in its blind spots and it can't out-duel a Corvette or any agile medium. It needs Imperial Duke rank, a large pad, and ~200M Cr. The Cutter wins fights by outlasting them, not by out-manoeuvring — in a turning duel it's at a real disadvantage.

At ~200M Cr the Cutter is the priciest hull in this batch, gated behind Imperial Duke — the top of the Imperial Navy rank ladder. A fully engineered combat fit, especially with a prismatic shield, pushes the all-in figure past 290M Cr.

It's the capstone of an Imperial combat career — and conveniently, the Imperial-rank grind you're already on toward the Achenar permit and Squire is the same ladder that eventually unlocks the Cutter at Duke.

The Imperial capstone

The Cutter sits at Imperial Duke, the top of the Imperial Navy. Your current Imperial-rank work toward Squire is the first stretch of the same ladder that ends here.

ARX Pre-Built Option

There is no combat-specific ARX pre-built built around the Imperial Cutter; buy and outfit it normally once you hold Duke rank.

Prismatic shields are the point

The Cutter's identity is its shield tank — a prismatic generator plus four Heavy-Duty boosters and multiple cell banks push it past 2,500 MJ, beyond anything else in the game. Build around the shield; the huge gun and reinforcement are the supporting cast.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Earn Imperial Duke, buy the hull, and fit the largest shield a class-8 bay allows plus reinforcement — even basic, it's nearly unkillable.

Arm the huge mount with a gimballed multi-cannon and spread beams/multi-cannons across the rest.

Load the eight utilities with shield boosters and add the fighter bay; the Cutter fields everything.

08

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for the shield-tank apex:

  • 1Shield Generator → prismatic — The Cutter's whole identity; the biggest, best shield is the priority. ~25M Cr
  • 2Power Plant → A — Power for seven guns, a huge shield, cells, SLF and eight utilities. ~30M Cr
  • 3Power Distributor → A — Sustain seven guns plus the shield-cell draw. ~22M Cr
  • 4Thrusters → A — Recover what mobility a 1,100t hull can manage. ~22M Cr
  • 5FSD → A — Move a vast warship between fights and turn-ins. ~20M Cr
Shield before guns

Unlike most warships, the Cutter is built shield-first — fit and engineer the biggest prismatic generator you can before touching weapons; the shield is the ship.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • High-intensity conflict zones. The Cutter sits in the hottest CZ and simply does not drop, grinding combat bonds indefinitely.
  • Hazardous RES. It clears the highest-value bounties at total leisure behind its shield wall.
  • Pirate massacre stacks. Stack missions and clear them from an essentially unkillable platform.
Advanced
  • Thargoid AX combat. Its shields and seven mounts make it a formidable AX tank with the right weapons and guardian modules.
  • Wing tanking. As the game's best shield tank it holds aggro and area-denies while wingmates strike — the ultimate wing anchor.
  • Combat-and-cargo. Vast internals let it haul a serious load while still fighting — capital-scale flexibility behind the shields.
Generic example systems

Any high-intensity CZ or Haz RES suits it; for AX, head to Thargoid-active systems. As a Gutamaya hull at the top of the Imperial Navy, it's the eventual endgame of your Imperial-rank journey beyond Squire and the Achenar permit.

12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Build shield-first — a prismatic generator and Heavy-Duty boosters are the whole point; the rest supports them.
  • Accept the agility; play it as an immovable shield fortress that out-lasts and out-guns, never as a duellist.
  • The prismatic shield generator needs Imperial-faction access to buy — your Imperial standing already provides this.
  • Fully engineering it is a major credit and material commitment; the shield, plant and distributor come first.
Verdict

The Imperial Cutter is the unsinkable battleship: the strongest shields in the game by far, a huge-mount seven-gun battery, and brisk straight-line speed for its size. The Corvette out-fights it and it can't turn to save itself — but for outlasting the hardest fights and tanking for a wing, nothing in the galaxy comes close. The Imperial combat capstone.

Briefing

The no-rank, low-cost AX medium — a tough workhorse Xeno hunter

The original Python makes a credible anti-Xeno medium: three large mounts for AX multi-cannons, two mediums for Gauss, ten optionals to layer Guardian hull and module reinforcement, and a genuinely tough frame — all with no rank gate, a medium pad and a hull under 56M Cr. It loses ground to the purpose-built mediums because it has no military slots, only four utilities, and turns too slowly to dodge an Interceptor's lightning the way a Chieftain or Krait can. It tanks the hits instead.

01

Role & Overview

Point the most flexible medium in the game at the Thargoids and you get a tough, accessible AX hunter. The Python brings three large hardpoints for AX/Guardian multi-cannons, two mediums for Gauss, four utilities and ten optional internals — deep enough to layer Guardian hull and module reinforcement, caustic kit and a strong bi-weave shield. With no rank gate, a medium pad and a hull under 56M Cr, it's one of the easiest AX warships to get into a Conflict Zone.

The reason it sits at 80 rather than higher on the AX medium ladder is specific and honest: the Python has no military slots, so every Guardian Module Reinforcement has to come out of an optional that would otherwise hold tank or cargo. It carries only four utility mounts — tight for a full Xeno-scanner, caustic-sink, heat-sink and shield-booster kit — and it turns ponderously, so it cannot dodge an Interceptor's lightning the way a Chieftain or Krait does. Its case is durability and value: it absorbs the hits a lighter hull evades.

Where this hull shines

Affordable, no-rank anti-Xeno work: standing in AX Conflict Zones, learning Interceptor hearts on a forgiving tank, and any AX sortie where a cheap, durable medium that also hauls or mines between fights matters more than peak evasion.

Four things make the Python a workable medium Xeno hunter:

  • Three large hardpoints. A heavy AX battery — three large AX/Guardian multi-cannons backed by two Gauss-capable mediums for heart damage.
  • Ten optional internals. Deep enough to layer Guardian hull and module reinforcement, a class-6 bi-weave and caustic kit — the room that compensates for having no military slots.
  • Strong base shield and heavy armour. The toughest of the classic combat mediums tanks the caustic and thermal damage that strips lighter AX hulls.
  • No rank, medium pad, low cost. No grind, lands anywhere, and re-roles to a hauler or miner between AX sorties.
The ceiling, stated honestly

The Python has no military slots, so every Guardian Module Reinforcement comes out of an optional, and it carries only four utilities — a tight budget for the full AX defensive kit. It's slow and turns poorly, so it cannot dodge an Interceptor's lightning like a Chieftain or Krait; it must absorb it. Its case is a tough, cheap, no-rank platform, not an evasive one.

At ~55.3M Cr the Python is mid-priced with no rank or permit gate — one of the cheapest viable AX warships. A full AX fit with AX/Guardian weapons, a capital-grade medium shield and layered Guardian reinforcement runs to roughly ~90M Cr A-rated, rising past ~120M Cr engineered, with a modest ~6M rebuy. The Guardian-tech grind for weapons and reinforcement is the real cost of admission, not credits.

It is an excellent first AX warship: cheap enough to rebuy without pain, tough enough to survive learning Interceptor hearts, and re-rollable to other work between sorties. If you want peak medium AX performance, the Chieftain or Krait dodge better for similar money; if you want one durable, no-rank hull that does AX among everything else, the Python is the value pick.

Cheap to fly, cheap to lose

No rank gate and a ~6M rebuy make the Python the forgiving way into AX. The Guardian unlocks for weapons and reinforcement are the grind that matters — the hull and credits are the easy part.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull, then unlock Guardian and AX weapons at the relevant sites — AX weapons cannot simply be bought from a shipyard.

Arm the two mediums with Gauss for heart damage and the three large mounts with AX multi-cannons.

Fit the largest bi-weave a class-6 bay allows and start layering Guardian reinforcement into the optionals; spread the four utilities across Xeno scanner, caustic sink, heat sink and a shield booster.

08

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

AX-fitting priority for the medium:

  • 1AX / Guardian weapons — Gauss on the mediums, AX multi-cannons on the three large mounts. material unlocks
  • 2Guardian reinforcement — Guardian MRP and HRP layered into the optionals; with no military slots this is the only way to harden modules. material unlocks
  • 3Power Distributor & Plant → A — Gauss and a strong shield are power- and capacitor-hungry; the class-7 cores matter most. ~16M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator & booster — A class-6 bi-weave with a thermal-resistant booster against caustic and thermal damage. ~7M Cr
  • 5Thrusters → A — Recover what agility the heavy hull allows, to keep Gauss on a heart. ~7M Cr
Hardening is the trade

Because the Python has no military slots, prioritise the Guardian MRP and HRP in the optionals over chasing extra cargo or shield depth — module hardening is what keeps it firing when an Interceptor's lightning would otherwise strip a lighter medium.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • AX Conflict Zones. A cheap, durable medium to grind AXCZ bonds while learning to read the swarm.
  • Learning Interceptors. Hold Gauss on a heart through Cyclops and Basilisk; the tank forgives early mistakes.
  • Caustic management. Run the caustic sink and decon limpet hard to scrub the corrosion lighter pilots panic over.
Advanced
  • Wing support. The durable medium that holds position while agile Chieftains and Kraits dart and harass.
  • Re-role between sorties. Swap optionals to haul materials or mine near the Maelstrom without buying a second hull.
  • Scout and Cyclops clearing. Three large AX guns clear Scouts and lighter Interceptors quickly and cheaply.
Generic example operations

Wherever Thargoids are fought — AXCZ, Interceptor hunts, Scout clearing near a Maelstrom. The Python is the affordable, no-rank medium you bring when durability and value matter more than peak evasion.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • No military slots — Guardian Module Reinforcement has to come out of the optionals, trading tank depth for module hardening.
  • Only four utilities: budget them deliberately across Xeno scanner, caustic sink, heat sink and a shield booster.
  • You can't dodge the lightning like a Chieftain — layer shield and Guardian hull tank and plan to absorb it.
  • It re-roles cheaply: swap optionals to haul or mine between AX sorties without buying a second ship.
Verdict

The original Python is the affordable, no-rank AX workhorse: three large AX mounts, two Gauss, ten optionals to layer Guardian reinforcement, and a tough frame — all on a medium pad for under 56M Cr. It scores 80 — behind the Chieftain, Krait and Challenger — because it has no military slots, only four utilities, and turns too slowly to dodge an Interceptor's lightning the way they do. It earns the rating as the cheap, durable hull that tanks the hits instead, and re-roles to anything else between fights.

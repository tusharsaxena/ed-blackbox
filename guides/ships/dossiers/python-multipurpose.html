01

Role & Overview

The Python is the medium-pad answer to 'one ship for everything.' Its three large hardpoints and strong shields make it a serious warship; its deep internals and ~294-tonne hold make it a defensible trader; it mines valuable cores while fighting off pirates; and it explores respectably. All of that on a medium pad that lands at the vast majority of stations — flexibility the capital all-rounders can't match.

It's slower than the Kraits and carries less than the dedicated freighters, but it does more things well than almost anything its size, and it's tough enough to survive whatever it's doing. For a single medium hull that fights, hauls, mines and explores — switchable by swapping modules — the Python has been a beloved benchmark for years. The medium fleet-in-one-ship.

Where this hull shines

Doing everything on a medium pad: switching between combat, defensible trading, core mining and exploration as needed, and any commander who wants maximum medium-pad versatility from one tough hull.

Four things make the Python the ultimate medium all-rounder:

  • Serious firepower. Three large hardpoints make it a genuine warship, not a token-armed trader.
  • Defensible capacity. ~294 tonnes of cargo and strong shields — it hauls and mines while fighting off pirates.
  • Deep, flexible internals. Room for any role's full kit — cargo, mining suite, exploration gear or combat tanking.
  • Medium-pad flexibility. Lands at most stations — far more accessible than the capital all-rounders.
The ceiling, stated honestly

It's slow, its jump range is modest (~45 LY), and specialists beat it everywhere — a Type-8 hauls more, a Mandalay explores far better, an FdL out-fights it. At ~55M Cr it's not cheap either. The Python's case is doing all of those things well in one tough medium hull; it's the best medium generalist, not a specialist.

Re-tune for the job

One balanced configuration — the Python's strength is swapping at a station: strip guns and HRPs for a fuel scoop, SRV and cargo to explore; fill with cargo to trade; add a mining suite (lasers, refinery, limpets) to mine. Keep role module sets in storage.

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fit a balanced multirole loadout: three large guns, strong shield, cargo and decent range.

A-rate the core internals first — they serve every role.

Build role-specific module sets (trader, miner, explorer) in storage to swap in.

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

  • 3Weapons — Three large gimballed guns for real combat capability. ~10M Cr
  • 4Power Distributor → A — Sustain guns and tools while laden. ~7M Cr
  • 5Role module sets — Cargo, mining suite, fuel scoop and SRV to swap. varies
A-rate the cores, then specialise

The Python's cores power every role — A-rate them first, then build storable module sets to swap between warship, trader, miner and explorer.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • A-rate the cores first — they power every role; then build storable module sets to swap.
  • Keep the three large guns — real firepower is what makes it more than a trader.
  • It's the best medium generalist, not a specialist — a Type-8, Mandalay or FdL beats it at the extremes.
  • It has combat, trading and mining manuals too — this one is for the do-everything build.
Verdict

The Python is the medium do-everything classic: a tough warship, defensible trader, capable miner and decent explorer in one medium-pad hull, switchable by swapping modules. Specialists edge it everywhere, but nothing its size matches its blend of firepower, capacity and toughness. The legendary medium jack-of-all-trades, and still one of the best ships in the game.

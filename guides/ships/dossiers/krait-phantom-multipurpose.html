01

Role & Overview

The Krait Phantom is the Krait Mk II's leaner sibling, and where the Mk II leans combat, the Phantom leans range. It drops a large hardpoint and the fighter bay for less mass and far more jump range — ~75 LY as an explorer — while keeping deep internals, good speed and a medium pad. The result is a do-everything medium that's a premier explorer first, and a capable hauler, miner and light combatant besides.

It carries ~190 tonnes, mounts two large guns for self-defence, and mines or explores excellently. It fights less hard than the Mk II or Python and hauls less than a Type-8, but no versatile medium travels as far or as comfortably while still doing everything else. For a commander who explores often but wants one hull that also trades, mines and defends itself, the Phantom is the ideal range-first all-rounder.

Where this hull shinesRange-leaning versatility: long expeditions that double as trade or mining trips, deep-space self-sufficiency, and any commander who wants a fast, far-jumping medium that also hauls, mines and defends itself.

Four things make the Phantom a great range-first all-rounder:

  • Premium jump range. ~75 LY as an explorer — far beyond any other versatile medium; expeditions are its forte.
  • Fast and roomy. Quick, with deep internals for a full expedition kit plus cargo or a mining suite.
  • Capable everywhere. ~190 tonnes of cargo, two large guns for defence, and good mining round it out.
  • Medium-pad and rank-free. Lands at most stations; anyone can fly one.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIt fights less hard than the Mk II or Python (two large hardpoints, no fighter), hauls less than a Type-8, and carries the least cargo of the top versatile mediums. Its combat is self-defence, not dominance. The Phantom's case is range-first versatility — superb for exploration-led play, second-best for combat-led.

At ~35.7M Cr the Phantom is the cheapest of the top versatile mediums, with no rank gate. Its versatility spreads the cost: one engineered hull explores, hauls, mines and defends itself, switchable by swapping modules.

Outfitting it across roles plus module sets brings the practical figure to around 68M Cr — a premier explorer that's also a capable all-rounder, for less than a Python or Mk II.

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fit a maxed FSD, two large guns, a strong shield and cargo or expedition kit.

A-rate the core internals first — they serve every role, especially the FSD.

Build role-specific module sets (explorer, trader, miner) in storage to swap in.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • A-rate the FSD first — range is the Phantom's signature; then the cores and module sets.
  • Its combat is self-defence, not dominance — two guns keep pirates off, not much more.
  • It out-ranges every versatile medium; the Python and Mk II out-fight and out-carry it.
  • It has an exploration manual too — this one is for the do-everything build.
VerdictThe Krait Phantom is the explorer's all-rounder: a premier long-range medium that also hauls, mines and defends itself, on a flexible pad. The Python and Krait Mk II fight harder and carry more, but none travels as far or explores as well while staying genuinely versatile. For range-first do-everything play, the Phantom is the ideal medium — and a community favourite for good reason.

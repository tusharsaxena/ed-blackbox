01

Role & Overview

The Cobra Mk III is the ship that taught a generation what a versatile spacecraft could be. Fast, nimble, cheap and balanced, it fights pirates, hauls cargo, explores respectably and runs any mission you throw at it — all on a small pad that lands anywhere, for around 200k credits. It does nothing at the top end, but it does almost everything well enough, and that breadth at that price is its enduring magic.

Against the medium all-rounders it carries less, fights softer and explores shorter, but it costs a fraction of any of them and remains gloriously fun to fly. For a new commander it's the perfect do-everything first ship; for a veteran it's a beloved cheap runabout that's always worth keeping in the hangar. Forty years on, the Cobra Mk III is still one of the great all-rounders.

Where this hull shinesCheap, fast, do-everything play: early-game versatility across combat, trade, exploration and missions, learning the game in one forgiving hull, and a beloved budget runabout for any commander.

Four things make the Cobra Mk III a great cheap all-rounder:

  • It does a bit of everything. Combat, trade, exploration and missions — all competently, on one cheap hull.
  • Fast and nimble. A 403 m/s boost and lovely handling make every role brisk and fun.
  • Balanced internals. Eight optionals carry guns, cargo, a scoop or a shield as the job demands.
  • Almost free and lands anywhere. ~208k credits, small pad — the accessible do-everything starter.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIt's small: light shields, only two utilities, modest cargo and firepower, and no role it excels at. The medium all-rounders out-do it everywhere — more guns, more cargo, more range, more tank. The Cobra's case is breadth and joy at a tiny price; it's the best cheap all-rounder, not a capable one by medium standards.

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull (~208k Cr) and fit a balanced loadout: a couple of guns, a shield and some cargo.

A-rate the core internals first — they serve every role.

Swap optionals (fuel scoop, cargo, SRV) to re-role as you like.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • It does nothing to the top, but everything well enough — and lands anywhere for next to nothing.
  • Lean on speed — the Cobra's pace makes every role brisk and fun.
  • Its modern successor the Cobra Mk V is roomier and tougher if you want a step up.
  • It has combat, exploration and trading manuals too — this one celebrates the all-rounder.
VerdictThe Cobra Mk III is the original all-rounder: cheap, fast, balanced, and able to do a bit of everything — combat, trade, exploration, missions — on a hull that lands anywhere for pocket change. The medium all-rounders outclass it in every measure but charm and price. Forty years on, it's still one of the great do-everything ships, and always worth a place in the hangar.

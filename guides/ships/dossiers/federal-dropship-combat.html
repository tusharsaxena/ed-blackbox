01

Role & Overview

The Federal Dropship is Core Dynamics' military transport turned light gunship — the ship Federal pilots first earn and the chassis the FAS and Gunship are built on. Five hardpoints (one large, four medium), two military slots and a heavy 540 armour base give it a respectable bite and a tough hide for very little money.

It's no thoroughbred: it's slow, turns ponderously and has weak base shields. But it's the cheapest Federal combat hull by a wide margin, requires only the lowest Federal rank to buy, and is durable enough to fight effectively while you climb toward the FAS, Gunship and ultimately the Corvette. As a value entry point and a rank-grind tool, it earns its place.

Where this hull shines

Entry-level conflict-zone and RES combat, pirate massacres, and the early stretch of the Federal-rank grind — an affordable, tough first warship that gets the job done without finesse.

Four things define the Dropship as a combat ship:

  • Five hardpoints. One large and four mediums give it more guns than its humble reputation suggests — a genuine main battery for the price.
  • Heavy armour and military slots. A 540 armour base plus two reinforcement bays make it tough enough to brawl despite its weak shields.
  • Lowest cost and rank gate. The cheapest Federal combat hull, on the lowest Federal rank — the accessible first rung of the ladder.
  • Deep, flexible optionals. Eight optional internals (more than the FAS or Gunship) give room for shields, cells and reinforcement.
The ceiling, stated honestly

It's slow, turns poorly and has the lowest base shield of the Federal trio — it can't dictate range, dodge, or shield-tank well, and its DPS trails the premium mediums. The FAS is faster and more aggressive; the Gunship hits far harder. The Dropship's case is purely value and accessibility — it's where you start, not where you stay.

At ~13.5M Cr the Dropship is the cheapest Federal combat hull, gated only by the low Midshipman rank. Its engineering bill is modest, and because it's the rank-grind ship, you'll likely outfit it just enough to fight effectively rather than maxing it out.

Spend on the cores and a bi-weave shield buffer; the Dropship's value is being good enough to win the fights that raise your Federal rank, cheaply.

Rung one of the ladder

The Dropship is the entry point of the Federal combat line. Buy it, fight in it, climb to the FAS and Gunship, and ultimately the Corvette — each fight in the Dropship advances that grind.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Earn Midshipman, buy the hull (~13.5M Cr), fit a shield and fill the military slots with hull reinforcement.

Arm the large mount with a gimballed multi-cannon and spread guns across the four mediums.

Even unengineered, the armour makes it a capable, cheap rank-grind brawler.

08

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for a budget armour-tank:

  • 1Power Plant → A — Power for five guns, a shield and reinforcement. ~3M Cr
  • 2Power Distributor → A — Sustain the guns and boost. ~2.5M Cr
  • 3Thrusters → A — Recover what mobility the heavy hull allows. ~2.5M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator → bi-weave — A regenerating buffer over the armour tank. ~2M Cr
  • 5FSD → A — Reach fights and Federal-rank turn-ins. ~3M Cr
Just enough to climb

The Dropship doesn't need maxing out — A-rate the cores and add a bi-weave and reinforcement, and it's good enough to win the fights that raise your Federal rank. Save the deep engineering for the Corvette it leads to.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Federal conflict zones. Fight for the Federation to earn combat bonds and Federal Navy rank — the Dropship's core purpose.
  • Hazardous RES. Its armour lets it brawl high-value targets despite the weak shields.
  • Pirate massacre stacks. Stack missions and clear them in steady RES or CZ sessions.
Advanced
  • The Federal rank climb. Grind Midshipman → CPO → Ensign and beyond, unlocking the FAS, Gunship and eventually the Corvette as you go.
  • Budget CZ farming. Cheap to rebuy, so you can fight aggressively without fearing the insurance bill.
  • Power-play combat. Clears enemy assets cheaply during fortification while you build Federal standing.
Generic example systems

Any Federal-aligned CZ or Haz RES suits it — favour Federation systems so combat bonds raise Federal rank. The Dropship's whole arc is the Federal grind toward a Corvette.

12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Treat it as the rank-grind ship it is — outfit it just enough to win, and save deep engineering for the Corvette.
  • Armour-tank it: the low shields mean Heavy-Duty reinforcement does the heavy lifting.
  • It's slow — pick fights you can win by attrition rather than ones that need you to dictate range.
  • Its cheap rebuy lets you fly aggressively while learning Federal CZ tactics.
Verdict

The Federal Dropship is the honest workhorse of the Federal combat line: cheap, tough and accessible, if slow and plain. It won't dazzle, but it's where the Federal journey begins — an affordable, durable brawler you fight in to earn the rank that unlocks the FAS, the Gunship and, ultimately, the Corvette. Rung one, and a sturdy one.

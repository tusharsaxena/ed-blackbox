Briefing

The fastest large hull in the game — a skirmisher, not a battleship

The Imperial Clipper trades hardpoints and tank for blistering speed and genuine agility. A capable, characterful large combat ship held back by only four hardpoints and a large-pad-only footprint — exceptional pace and handling for the class, mid-pack firepower and tank. Fly it fast, close on your terms, and disengage before the return fire lands.

01

Role & Overview

The Imperial Clipper is the speed merchant of the large class — a long, elegant Gutamaya hull that boosts to nearly 390 m/s and turns far better than its size suggests. As a combat ship it fields two large and two medium hardpoints, decent shields and a roomy nine-slot internal bay, and it uses its pace to fight on its own terms: close fast, strike, and disengage before the return fire lands.

Its quirks are real. Only four hardpoints cap its firepower below the other large warships, and despite its agility it requires a large pad — an awkward combination, since it has medium-ship handling but can't use medium pads. It also needs Imperial rank to buy. But as a fast, affordable, good-looking skirmisher that flies like nothing else its size, the Clipper has a devoted following.

Where this hull shines

Fast hit-and-run combat: skirmishing in conflict zones and RES, running down or escaping targets at will, and any fight where speed and repositioning beat standing and grinding. Also a fine fast Imperial-rank brawler.

Four things define the Clipper as a combat ship:

  • Unmatched speed. The fastest large ship in the game — 388 m/s boost stock, well past 450 engineered — lets it dictate every engagement's range and escape any losing fight.
  • Surprising agility. It turns far better than its size suggests, holding guns on target like a heavy medium rather than a lumbering capital.
  • Two large hardpoints. Enough main-battery punch to hit hard on a fast pass, backed by two mediums.
  • Roomy and affordable. Nine optionals for shields and reinforcement, and a hull cheaper than most mediums — a bargain large ship.
The ceiling, stated honestly

Four hardpoints and no military slots cap its firepower and tank well below the other large warships, and the large-pad-only footprint is a genuine nuisance for a ship that handles like a medium. It needs Imperial rank too. The Clipper wins on speed and style, not on firepower or toughness — it's a skirmisher, not a battleship.

At ~21M Cr the Clipper is remarkably cheap for a large ship — less than many mediums — gated only by the modest Imperial Baron rank. Its shallow hardpoints keep the combat outfitting bill low.

It's a fine fast brawler to fly while climbing Imperial rank: the same grind you're on toward Squire and the Achenar permit will unlock and then surpass the Baron rank it needs, and the Clipper makes the journey quick and stylish.

Cheap, fast, Imperial

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Earn Baron, buy the hull (~21M Cr), and fit a stock shield generator plus a single shield booster — bulkheads stay stock Lightweight Alloy, and every reinforcement and spare utility slot stays empty until A-rating.

Arm the four mounts buy-only with gimballed pulse lasers and multi-cannons; the shield-stripping beam laser and the buy-only Military Grade Composite bulkhead arrive with the A-rated fit.

Lean on the speed immediately — close, strike and disengage rather than brawling.

08

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for a fast skirmisher:

  • 1Thrusters → 6A — Speed is the Clipper's whole game; A-rate thrusters first. ~4M Cr
  • 2Power Distributor → 6A — Sustain two large guns plus boost. ~3M Cr
  • 3Power Plant → 6A — Headroom for guns, shields and utilities. ~3M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator → 6C bi-weave — A fast-regen buffer for a hit-and-run hull (a size-6 bi-weave under-fills the size-7 slot to save mass — defensible while the thesis is speed). ~3M Cr
  • 5FSD → 5A — Reach fights and Imperial-rank turn-ins. ~3M Cr
  • 6Bulkheads → Military Grade Composite — Buy-only hull upgrade for a real survivability floor; the size-4/4/2/1 internals fill with Hull Reinforcement Packages (the size-1 slot carries an HRP, not an exploration scanner) and two Shield Cell Banks back the bi-weave. ~2M Cr
Protect the speed

The Clipper lives by its pace — A-rate and engineer thrusters before anything else so it always dictates range and can escape a losing fight.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Skirmishing RES and CZs. Use the speed to pick targets, strike on a fast pass, and reset — never get pinned.
  • Imperial conflict zones. Fight for the Empire to earn combat bonds and Imperial rank together.
  • Bounty hunting on the move. Run down fleeing targets nothing else its size can catch.
Advanced
  • Hit-and-run CZ play. Engineered, the Clipper darts through a conflict zone striking priority targets and slipping away before focus-fire lands.
  • Imperial-rank brawling. Fast and cheap, it makes the Imperial rank climb toward Duke (and a Cutter) quick and enjoyable.
  • Fast response and patrol. Its speed makes it ideal for reaching distant signals and threats quickly.
Generic example systems

Any Imperial-aligned CZ or RES suits it; favour Imperial systems so combat bonds raise Imperial rank toward the Achenar permit and beyond. The Clipper's speed shines on routes that punish slower ships.

12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Fly it as a skirmisher — speed and repositioning are its defence; don't try to out-tank a battleship.
  • Remember the large-pad-only footprint despite medium-ship handling — plan docking accordingly.
  • Four hardpoints mean every gun counts; mix multi-cannons for hull and a beam for shields.
  • It pairs well with your Imperial-rank grind — fast, cheap, and on the same ladder that leads to the Cutter.
Verdict

The Imperial Clipper is the fast skirmisher of the large class: the quickest big ship in the game, agile enough to fly like a medium, beautiful and cheap. Only four hardpoints, no military slots and a large-pad-only footprint keep it mid-pack for raw combat — but for hit-and-run flair and value, and as a stylish Imperial-rank brawler, nothing its size flies like it.

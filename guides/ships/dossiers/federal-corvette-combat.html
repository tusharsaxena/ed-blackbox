01

Role & Overview

The Federal Corvette is Core Dynamics' masterpiece and the undisputed king of combat. Two huge hardpoints — a count no other ship in the game can field — anchor an eight-mount battery, backed by a 666 base armour hull, two military slots, eleven optional internals, eight utilities and a ship-launched-fighter bay. It is a battleship that hits harder than anything else flying.

What sets it apart from the other capitals is agility. For a 900-tonne warship the Corvette turns astonishingly well, letting it hold its huge guns on target the way a much smaller ship would. The Imperial Cutter out-shields it and the Anaconda is more flexible, but for the single job of destroying other ships, nothing matches the Corvette. The catch is the climb: it sits at the very top of the Federal Navy rank ladder.

Where this hull shines

Elite combat at every scale: the toughest conflict zones, Hazardous RES, pirate massacres, Thargoid AX, PvP and wing fights. Anywhere the goal is simply to out-gun and destroy, the Corvette is the answer.

Four things make the Corvette the combat apex:

  • Two huge hardpoints. Unique in the game — a pair of huge mounts plus four more guns delivers alpha and sustained DPS no other ship can equal.
  • Capital agility. It turns far better than any other large warship, keeping its huge guns on target like a medium — the single biggest reason it tops the rankings.
  • Battleship tank with military slots. 666 armour, ~516 MJ shields, two military slots and deep optionals make it as hard to kill as it is to survive.
  • Eight utilities and an SLF bay. A full defensive utility suite plus a launched fighter make it a one-ship battlefleet.
The ceiling, stated honestly

The costs are access and scale, not capability. It demands Federal Rear Admiral — one of the longest rank grinds in the game — needs a large pad, costs ~183M Cr before outfitting, and is expensive to engineer fully. The Cutter out-shields it and the Anaconda needs no rank. But for pure combat, the Corvette has no superior; the only question is whether you'll do the grind.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Earn Rear Admiral, buy the hull, and fit a stock 7E Shield Generator plus a 5D Hull Reinforcement in the first military slot — even E-rated cores and a basic shield make it a fortress.

Arm the two huge mounts with 4A Multi-Cannons (Gimballed) and fill out the battery buy-only: a 3C Beam Laser for shield-stripping, plus a 2F Multi-Cannon, a 2F Pulse Laser and a 1G Multi-Cannon. The last small hardpoint stays empty until A-rating.

Only one 0A Shield Booster goes on buy-only. The fighter bay, the second military slot, the rest of the utilities and the deep optional reinforcement all wait for the A-rated pass — the buy-only fit is a working warship, not yet the full battlefleet.

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for the apex warship:

  • 1Power Distributor → 8A — Two huge guns plus six more and boost demand the class-8 distributor first. ~22M Cr
  • 2Power Plant → 8A — Power for eight guns, a big shield, cells, SLF and eight utilities. ~28M Cr
  • 3Thrusters → 7A — Preserve the capital agility that makes the Corvette the apex. ~22M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator → 7C bi-weave (or 7A prismatic) — A capital-scale shield is the first tank layer. ~18M Cr
  • 5FSD → 6A — Move a 900t warship between fights and turn-ins. ~18M Cr
Thrusters are the secret

The Corvette's edge over other capitals is agility — A-rate and engineer thrusters so a 900-tonne hull keeps turning like a far smaller ship and holds its huge guns on target.

Recommended order

  • 1Distributor and plant to G5 first — two huge guns plus six more and a fighter are immensely power-hungry.
  • 2Thrusters to G5 to keep the capital agility that defines the ship.

Material intensity (qualitative)

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • High-intensity conflict zones. The Corvette dominates the toughest CZs outright — the best combat-bond rates in the game.
  • Hazardous RES. It clears the highest-value bounties effortlessly while shrugging off return fire.
  • Pirate massacre stacks. Stack missions and clear them from the most lethal platform available.
Advanced
  • Thargoid AX combat. With AX/Guardian weapons and its huge mounts, eight utilities and capital tank, the Corvette is a premier anti-Thargoid warship.
  • PvP and CG combat. Two huge hardpoints and capital agility make it a feared player-combat and community-goal hull.
  • Wing flagship. As the apex warship with an SLF and full utility suite, it anchors and dominates any wing engagement.
Generic example systems

Any high-intensity CZ or Haz RES suits it; for AX, head to Thargoid-active systems. As the Federal capstone it's the endgame combat flagship your Dropship→FAS→Gunship grind builds toward.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Thrusters first, always — the Corvette's edge is agility no other capital has; protect it.
  • The two huge mounts are the ship — build everything else to keep them firing and on target.
  • Fill both military slots and the deep optionals with reinforcement; layer shield and hull tanks together.
  • Fully engineering it is a major credit and material commitment — prioritise distributor, plant, thrusters, shield and the huge guns.
Verdict

The Federal Corvette is the most lethal ship in Elite Dangerous: two huge hardpoints, eight mounts, battleship armour and — uniquely — capital agility that keeps it all on target. The Cutter out-shields it and the Anaconda needs no rank, but nothing out-fights it. Earn the Rear Admiral rank, and the best warship in the game is yours.

01

Role & Overview

The Imperial Cutter is the rare ship that hauls like a freighter and survives like a warship. Its ten optional internals carry nearly 800 tonnes — second only to the Panther and Type-9 — while its shields, the strongest in the game, and eight utility mounts make it almost impossible to rob. And uniquely among big haulers, it's fast: a 323 m/s boost lets it simply outrun pirates a Type-9 would have to fight.

That combination — huge cargo, apex shields, real speed — makes it the premier ship for high-value and dangerous trade routes, where a Type-9 would be a sitting duck. The costs are the Imperial Duke rank and a ~200M Cr price tag. But for a commander hauling a fortune through contested space, the Cutter carries more, more safely, than anything except the slower, flimsier-by-comparison super-haulers.

Where this hull shinesHigh-value and dangerous-route trading: carrying fortunes through hostile or contested space, smuggling, Community Goal hauling under threat, and any run where surviving (or outrunning) interdiction matters as much as capacity.

Four things make the Cutter the best defensible hauler:

  • Huge cargo. Nearly 800 tonnes — capacity rivalling the dedicated super-haulers, on a ship that also fights.
  • The strongest shields in the game. A prismatic Cutter is almost impossible to break — your cargo is safe even under sustained attack.
  • Fast for a large ship. A 323 m/s boost lets it outrun pirates other haulers must stand and fight.
  • Eight utilities. A full shield-booster and point-defence suite makes it a fortress that happens to carry cargo.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIt needs Imperial Duke rank — a long grind — costs ~200M Cr, is large-pad-bound, and carries a little less than the Panther or Type-9. A keeping-its-shield trade fit also sacrifices some cargo slots to defence. The Cutter's case is defensible, fast bulk hauling; for pure maximum tonnage or value, the Panther or cheaper Type-9 win.

At ~200M Cr and behind the Imperial Duke rank, the Cutter is a major commitment. A defensible trade fit with a prismatic shield pushes the all-in figure past 270M Cr.

Conveniently, your Imperial-rank journey toward Squire and the Achenar permit is the same ladder that eventually reaches Duke — so the trading Cutter sits at the far end of a grind you're already on, and the same hull doubles as a combat flagship.

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Earn Duke, buy the hull, and fit a big shield plus cargo racks — protected hauling from the start.

Load the eight utilities with shield boosters and point defence.

A-rate thrusters and FSD so the laden ship keeps its speed advantage.

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for a defensible hauler:

  • 1Shield Generator → prismatic — The Cutter's whole edge; the best shield protects the cargo. ~25M Cr
  • 2Thrusters → A — Keep the speed that lets it outrun pirates. ~20M Cr
  • 3FSD → A — Laden jump range for route efficiency. ~20M Cr
  • 4Power Plant → A — Power for the big shield and utilities. ~25M Cr
  • 5Power Distributor → A — Sustain boost and shield cells. ~18M Cr
Shield and speed protect the cargoUnlike a Type-9, the Cutter survives interdiction — prioritise the prismatic shield and A-rated thrusters so it can both out-tank and outrun trouble.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • High-value commodity runs. Carry fortunes through contested space that would doom a Type-9.

  • Community Goal hauling under threat. Top delivery boards on routes others can't survive.
Advanced
  • Dangerous-route specialist. Anarchy systems, pirate lanes and conflict zones — it hauls where lesser traders won't go.
  • Dual-role flagship. The same hull is a combat apex — trade one week, fight the next.
  • Powerplay logistics. Move fortification and undermining cargo through hostile space safely.
Generic example routesAny high-value loop, especially through dangerous space, suits it. As a Gutamaya hull it pairs with your Imperial-rank journey — the trading Cutter is one reward at the end of the Duke grind.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Protection is the point — a prismatic shield and full booster suite let it carry fortunes safely.
  • Keep the speed — A-rated thrusters mean it outruns what it can't out-tank.
  • It carries a bit less than the Panther/Type-9 but is far safer; choose it for dangerous routes.
  • Mind the large pad and the Duke rank gate — both are part of the cost.
VerdictThe Imperial Cutter is the armoured fortune-carrier: nearly 800 tonnes of cargo behind the strongest shields in the game, and fast enough to outrun what it can't out-tank. The Panther and Type-9 haul more, but neither can carry a fortune through hostile space and live. For defensible, dangerous-route bulk trading, the Cutter is unmatched — and it's a combat flagship besides.

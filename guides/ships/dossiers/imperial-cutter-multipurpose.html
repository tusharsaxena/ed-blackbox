01

Role & Overview

The Imperial Cutter is what happens when a luxury liner is built like a warship. It carries the strongest shields in the game, hauls nearly 800 tonnes, mounts a huge plus two large and four medium hardpoints for serious firepower, and — uniquely for so large a ship — boosts past 320 m/s. It trades, fights, mines and even passenger-cruises at the very top end, all in one opulent hull.

Its weaknesses as an all-rounder are specific: a modest jump range makes it a poor explorer, and it needs the long Imperial Duke grind. But for combat-and-trade versatility — hauling a fortune one day, dominating a conflict zone the next, behind apex shields and at unexpected speed — little compares. It's the Imperial endgame flagship, and the natural reward at the top of the rank ladder you're already climbing.

Where this hull shines

Top-end combat-and-trade versatility: bulk hauling a fortune through danger, dominating conflict zones, defended mining, and luxury passenger cruising — all in one apex-shielded Imperial flagship.

Four things make the Cutter a luxury capital all-rounder:

  • Apex shields. The strongest in the game — it survives anything while trading, fighting or mining.
  • Huge cargo plus firepower. Nearly 800 tonnes and a huge-hardpoint warship's guns in one hull.
  • Fast for a capital. A 323 m/s boost lets it escape or chase where other big ships can't.
  • Eight utilities and two military slots. Room to be excellent at whatever role it's fitted for.
The ceiling, stated honestly

Its jump range is modest, so it's a poor explorer — the one role it can't do well. It needs the long Imperial Duke grind, costs ~200M Cr, and is large-pad-bound. The Cutter's case is top-end combat-and-trade versatility behind the best shields; for exploration or rank-free access, the Anaconda is the better generalist.

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Earn Duke, buy the hull, and fit a balanced loadout: prismatic shield, real guns and cargo.

A-rate the core internals; load the eight utilities with shield boosters.

Build role-specific module sets (trader, combat, miner) to swap.

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for a luxury all-rounder:

  • 1Shield Generator → prismatic — Apex shields are the Cutter's signature; do this first. ~25M Cr
  • 2Core internals → A — Plant, thrusters, FSD and distributor serve every role. ~50M Cr
  • 3Weapons — Huge plus large gimballed guns for real combat. ~20M Cr
  • 4Power Distributor → A — Sustain guns and shield cells. ~18M Cr
  • 5Role module sets — Cargo racks, mining suite to swap. varies
Shields first, then everything

The prismatic shield is what makes the Cutter survive any role — fit it first, then A-rate the cores and build storable module sets for combat and trade.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Top-end combat and trade. Dominate conflict zones or haul a fortune through danger, behind apex shields.
  • Defended bulk hauling. Carry the maximum cargo a medium-rank pilot can dream of, safely.
  • Powerplay and CG flagship. A capital hull for high-stakes logistics and combat alike.
Advanced
  • Luxury passenger cruising. Fit cabins for a comfortable, well-shielded liner — it has a passenger-capable frame.
  • Re-role on demand. Swap module sets between combat, trade and mining.
  • Imperial endgame. The flagship reward at the top of the rank ladder you're already on.
Generic example uses

Whatever the week demands except deep exploration — bounties, a fortune-haul, a defended mining run, a luxury cruise. As a Gutamaya hull it caps your Imperial-rank journey beyond the Achenar permit.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Fit the prismatic shield first — apex survivability is the Cutter's identity in every role.
  • It's a poor explorer — leave deep-space range to an Anaconda or Mandalay.
  • A-rate the cores and build storable module sets to swap between combat and trade.
  • It has combat and trading manuals too — this one is for the do-most-things build.
Verdict

The Imperial Cutter is the luxury capital all-rounder: apex shields, nearly 800 tonnes of cargo, real firepower and surprising speed in one opulent Imperial hull. Its weak jump range keeps it from the Anaconda's all-round crown, and the Duke grind gates it — but for top-end combat-and-trade versatility behind the best shields in the game, the Cutter is the Imperial endgame flagship.

01

Role & Overview

The Corsair is Gutamaya's contemporary answer to the medium combat question, and it answers it with breadth. Six hardpoints — three large and three medium — give it a heavy, flexible main battery, while ten optional internals (deep for a combat hull) let it tank, sustain and even carry cargo without compromising its guns. Imperial design means it's quick and handsome with it.

Where the Krait leans on an SLF bay and the Fer-de-Lance on a huge hardpoint, the Corsair simply does everything well: lots of guns, lots of room, plenty of speed. It has no military slots, so its tanking comes from those generous optionals rather than dedicated bays — but the sheer count of internals means it can still shield- or hull-tank deeply. It's the modern, do-it-all combat medium.

Where this hull shinesConflict zones, Hazardous RES, pirate massacres and mixed combat play — anywhere a fast, six-gun medium with deep internals to tank and sustain is wanted, especially for pilots who like options.

Four things make the Corsair a top-tier modern combat medium:

  • Six hardpoints. Three large plus three medium — a heavy, flexible main battery that matches or beats most mediums for total firepower.
  • Ten optional internals. Exceptionally deep for a combat hull, with three class-6 bays — tank with shields and cell banks, or hull-tank with reinforcement, without giving up guns.
  • Imperial speed and class-7 power. Fast and agile with a strong plant and distributor to feed six guns plus boost — no power starvation.
  • Modern flexibility. The room to carry cargo, scanners or extra defences means it adapts to mixed combat play better than narrower specialists.
The ceiling, stated honestlyNo huge hardpoint, no fighter bay, no military slots — it lacks the single standout feature of the Fer-de-Lance, Krait or Alliance hulls. Its tanking, while deep, comes from optionals it must share with everything else. The Corsair wins on balance and breadth, not on any one peak stat; and at ~77M Cr it's a premium price for that breadth.

At ~76.9M Cr the Corsair is one of the priciest combat mediums, but it carries no rank or permit gate — just credits. Its deep internals mean a full combat fit (six engineered guns, shields, cells, reinforcement) adds up, putting it among the more expensive mediums to bring to its ceiling.

You pay for breadth and modernity. If you want one combat medium that's fast, heavily armed and flexible, the Corsair justifies its price; if budget matters, the Alliance hulls deliver most of the capability for far less.

Premium, no rankThe Corsair is expensive but rank-free — the modern, credits-only route to a six-gun combat medium with large-ship internal depth.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fit the largest shield a class-6 bay allows plus a hull reinforcement — RES-ready immediately.

Arm the three large mounts with gimballed multi-cannons and the mediums with support weapons.

Use a couple of optionals for cargo or scanners early; the depth means you needn't commit everything to combat at once.

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for a six-gun combat medium:

  • 1Power Distributor → A — Six guns plus boost demand the class-7 distributor first. ~8M Cr
  • 2Power Plant → A — Headroom for six guns, shields and utilities. ~10M Cr
  • 3Thrusters → A — Preserve the Imperial speed and agility. ~8M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator → bi-weave — Fast regen suits a brawler that re-engages. ~6M Cr
  • 5FSD → A — Reach fights and turn-ins faster. ~6M Cr
Distributor and plant firstSix guns are power-hungry — A-rate the class-7 distributor and plant before weapons, then build the tank into the deep optionals.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Conflict zones. Six guns and deep tanking let the Corsair dominate medium and high CZs for combat bonds.
  • Hazardous RES. Heavy firepower clears high-value bounties fast while the deep internals keep you safe.
  • Pirate massacre stacks. Stack missions and clear them in one well-armed session.
Advanced

  • High-intensity CZ farming. Engineered, it thrives in the toughest conflict zones.
  • Power-play combat. Clears enemy assets quickly while you grind Imperial standing — and as a Gutamaya hull it fits the Imperial aesthetic.
Generic example systemsAny Haz RES or CZ suits it. As an Imperial-built hull it pairs naturally with your Imperial-rank grind — CZ and massacre payouts double as standing progress.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Exploit the depth: decide shield- or hull-tank and use the ten optionals fully — few combat mediums can tank this hard.
  • Six guns reward a mixed loadout — multi-cannons for hull, beams for shields, one Corrosive to amplify it all.
  • It's fast for a six-gun medium — use the speed to control range as well as the firepower to win it.
  • At its price, it competes with large warships on capability; weigh the premium against an Alliance hull if budget matters.
VerdictThe Corsair is the modern, do-everything combat medium: six guns, ten optionals and Imperial speed, with no rank gate. It lacks a signature trick — no huge mount, fighter bay or military slots — but its sheer breadth of firepower, room and pace makes it one of the most capable all-round combat mediums in the game, for those willing to pay the premium.

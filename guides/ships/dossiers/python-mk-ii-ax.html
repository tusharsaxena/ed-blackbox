Python Mk IIAX Combat

Series Ships Updated 2026-06-25
Briefing

The four-large-mount medium turned on the Thargoids — most AX firepower on a medium pad

No rank gate, a medium pad, and four large hardpoints — a big-gun count usually reserved for capitals — make the Python Mk II the hardest-hitting AX medium credits can buy. Two Gauss on the mediums and four large Guardian/AX guns put more heart-and-hull damage downrange than any other mid-pad hull. It scores 84 rather than higher because it carries zero military slots and only six optionals: Guardian Module Reinforcement, hull, shield and caustic kit all fight over the same six bays, so the module hardening that AX demands comes at the cost of the tank. It out-guns the agile leaders but can't both dodge like a Chieftain and harden like a Corvette.

01

Role & Overview

Point the best credit-bought medium gunship at the Thargoids and you get the hardest-hitting AX medium flying. The Python Mk II keeps the Python name but is a focused warship: four large hardpoints — a big-mount count most mediums never see — plus two mediums, six utilities, and a class-6 plant and distributor sized to run all of it. Since no huge AX weapon exists, four large mounts is effectively the practical AX firepower ceiling, and the Mk II reaches it from a medium pad with no rank grind.

The reason it sits at 84 rather than the top of the AX ladder is specific and honest: it has zero military slots and only six optional internals, by design. In AX, Thargoid lightning strips modules, so Guardian Module Reinforcement is near-mandatory — but on the Mk II it competes for the same six bays as the shield generator, hull reinforcement, caustic kit and decon limpets. You cannot both harden like a military-slotted hull and stay light enough to dodge like a Chieftain. The agile mediums with military slots (Chieftain, Challenger) and the high-DPS Krait Mk II edge ahead on that survivability balance; the Mk II answers with raw firepower and pad-and-credit access.

Where this hull shines

Firepower-led AX work: ending Interceptors fast with four large guns and two Gauss, AX Conflict Zones where you kill the Scout swarm quickly, and any commander who wants top-tier anti-Xeno damage without a navy rank grind. Fly it aggressively — speed and alpha, not attrition.

Four things make the Mk II a top-tier AX medium:

  • Four large hardpoints. Four large Guardian/AX guns plus two mediums — the biggest AX weapon array of any medium, and since no huge AX gun exists, the practical firepower ceiling.
  • Two Gauss-capable mediums. The Guardian Gauss Cannon is a medium-class fixed weapon; two of them on the mediums supply the heart damage that ends Interceptors.
  • Power to run it. A class-6 plant and class-6 distributor fire the whole array and boost without the brown-outs that cripple thirstier Gauss boats.
  • Speed and agility. 256/345 m/s and medium handling let it reposition and partly dodge lightning — far more evasion than any AX capital.
The ceiling, stated honestly

Zero military slots and only six optionals is the real constraint. Guardian Module Reinforcement — the hardening that keeps modules alive under Thargoid lightning — competes for the same bays as the shield, hull reinforcement, caustic sink storage and decon limpets. You cannot field a deep module-hardened tank and stay as light as a Chieftain. The Mk II's case is firepower and evasion, not the attritional module-tank a Corvette or Challenger brings.

The hull is ~64.7M Cr — not cheap — but it carries no rank or permit gate, so it's open to any commander with the credits. A full AX fit with Guardian/AX weapons, a capable bi-weave and Guardian reinforcement runs to ~95M Cr A-rated, rising past ~120M Cr engineered, with a modest ~6M rebuy to budget for. The real cost beyond credits is the Guardian-tech and AX-weapon unlock grind shared by every anti-Xeno ship.

That makes it the most accessible top-tier AX platform: no Federal or Imperial grind, just money. If you want elite anti-Xeno firepower without a navy ladder, this is the medium — though new AX pilots should still learn the dance in a cheaper, more forgiving Chieftain before committing this much.

No rank gate

Unlike the Corvette (Federal Rear Admiral) and Cutter (Imperial Duke), the Python Mk II asks only for credits and the standard Guardian/AX unlocks. The medium pad and modest rebuy make it the easy-access big-firepower AX hull.

05

ARX Pre-Built Option

Faulcon DeLacy offers the Python Mk II as an ARX pre-built package, but no variant is AX-specific. If you took one, never strip the stock cores — add to open slots and engineer the cores in place — then unlock Guardian and AX weapons at the relevant sites and outfit it for anti-Xeno work normally. For most commanders, simply buying the hull and fitting it AX is cleaner.

Six bays, no military — harden where you can

With no military slots, Guardian Module Reinforcement has to live in the civilian optionals alongside the shield and hull kit. 07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull — no rank needed — then unlock Guardian and AX weapons at the relevant sites; there is no off-the-shelf AX gun to slot first.

Out of the box only the stock cores come fitted: the E-rated Power Plant, Thrusters, Frame Shift Drive, Life Support, Power Distributor and Sensors, plus the stock 4C Fuel Tank. Every hardpoint, utility and optional internal stays empty (— in the buy-only column).

The reason is that the whole AX suite — the two Gauss, the four large Guardian/AX guns, the bi-weave shield, and the Guardian hull and module reinforcement — is unlock-gated, so there is nothing to slot buy-only. Those bays wait for the A-rated pass once the Guardian and AX unlocks are done.

08

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

AX-fitting priority for a firepower-led medium:

  • 1AX / Guardian weapons — Gauss on the mediums, large Guardian/AX guns on the four big mounts. material unlocks
  • 2Power Distributor & Plant → A — Gauss and four large guns are immensely capacitor- and power-hungry; this comes before tank. ~22M Cr
  • 3Shield Generator → bi-weave + boosters — Fast-regen bi-weave with thermal-resistant boosters against caustic and thermal damage. ~12M Cr
  • 4Guardian reinforcement — Pack Guardian module and hull reinforcement into the remaining optionals; with no military slots, this is the only hardening you get. material unlocks
  • 5Thrusters → A — Preserve the speed and agility that let it dodge lightning instead of soaking it. ~9M Cr
Distributor before tank

Four large guns plus two Gauss will brown out anything less than an A-rated, Charge-Enhanced distributor. A-rate and engineer it before chasing reinforcement — the firepower is the Mk II's whole AX case.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • AX Conflict Zones. Four large guns and two Gauss clear the Scout swarm fast, then turn on the Interceptors.
  • Interceptor kills. Hold Gauss on a heart through Cyclops to Medusa; the alpha shortens every kill.
  • Dodge, don't soak. Use the speed to break line-of-sight from lightning rather than tanking it — you lack the module-tank to absorb it.
Advanced
  • Wing damage-dealer. Bring the firepower while a tankier hull anchors and holds aggro.
  • Maelstrom & Titan operations. Fast, hard-hitting sorties where you kill and reposition rather than hold a fixed line.
  • Hydra and Basilisk hunts. The four-large array shortens the toughest Interceptor fights — fly aggressively and keep moving.
Generic example operations

Wherever Thargoids are fought — AXCZ, Interceptor hunts, Maelstrom and Titan ops. The Mk II is the medium you bring when the plan is to out-damage the fight, from a pad and rank anyone can reach.

12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • No military slots: Guardian Module Reinforcement competes with the shield and hull for the six optionals — decide your harden-vs-firepower split before you build.
  • Gauss is a medium-class weapon, so you get two of them; the four large mounts carry Enhanced AX multi-cannons, a plasma charger and a shard cannon.
  • You can't out-tank a Corvette or Challenger — use the 256/345 m/s speed to break from lightning rather than absorb it.
  • Manage heat hard with Gauss, run caustic sinks and a decon limpet, and keep the distributor topped — it's the only thing feeding six AX guns.
Verdict

The Python Mk II is the hardest-hitting AX medium credits can buy: four large mounts and two Gauss put more anti-Xeno damage downrange than any other mid-pad hull, with no rank gate to earn it. It scores 84 — behind the agile, military-slotted Chieftain, Krait Mk II and Challenger — because zero military slots and only six optionals mean the module hardening AX demands always costs the tank. Fly it for firepower and evasion, not attrition, and it's an outstanding anti-Xeno choice.

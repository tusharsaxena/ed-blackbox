01

Role & Overview

The Alliance Crusader is the trio's crewed gunboat. It shares the chassis, the three military slots and the tough hull, but its defining features are a built-in ship-launched-fighter bay and a three-seat cockpit for multicrew. It's designed to fight as a team: you fly and shoot, a gunner mans turrets or the SLF, and a launched fighter adds a third angle of attack.

The cost is pace. The Crusader is the slowest of the three Alliance hulls and carries the lowest base shield, so flown solo it's the least dynamic of the family. But with a fighter deployed and a gunner aboard, it becomes a force multiplier — effectively two-and-a-half ships in one hull — which is exactly the niche the Chieftain and Challenger don't fill.

Where this hull shinesCrewed and wing combat: multicrew sessions with a friend gunning, NPC-fighter support in CZs and RES, and any fight where a launched fighter splitting enemy attention turns the tide.

Four things define the Crusader as a combat ship:

  • A fighter bay. Launch an SLF to split enemy fire and add a second damage source — a force multiplier the other two Alliance hulls don't have.
  • Three crew seats. Multicrew lets a friend or NPC gun for you or fly the fighter, multiplying your effective firepower and awareness.
  • Three military slots. The Alliance tank signature — dedicated reinforcement bays that toughen the hull without eating shield or cargo room.
  • Six weapon mounts. One large, two medium and three small — a respectable spread for the pilot while crew and fighter add more.
The ceiling, stated honestlySolo, the Crusader is the weakest Alliance hull — slowest, lowest shield, and its real strength (crew and SLF) is partly idle without a gunner. Its DPS and agility trail the Chieftain. It only reaches its potential as a crewed or fighter-supported platform; if you fly alone and want the best Alliance combat ship, take the Chieftain.

At ~22M Cr the Crusader sits between the Chieftain and Challenger in price, with no rank or permit gate. The fighter bay and a good NPC pilot are part of the build cost — budget for them, since the SLF is central to the ship's value.

A full combat fit with three military-slot reinforcements, a shield, and the fighter bay adds up, but stays well under a large fighter-carrier's cost — the Crusader is the budget route to crewed combat.

Budget the fighterThe SLF bay and a high-rank NPC pilot are integral, not optional, on the Crusader — the ship is built around them. Factor both into the build from the start.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fit a shield, fill the military slots with hull reinforcement, and add the fighter bay early.

Arm the large and medium mounts for yourself; put a turret or two on the smalls for a gunner.

Even solo with an NPC fighter pilot it's CZ-ready; with a human gunner it's far stronger.

A-rating priority for a crewed gunboat:

  • 3Thrusters → A — Recover what speed the heavy hull allows. ~3M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator → bi-weave — A buffer over the hull tank; the base shield is low. ~3M Cr
  • 5FSD → A — Reach fights and turn-ins. ~3M Cr
Power for the fighter bayAn SLF draws meaningful power — A-rate the plant before fitting the fighter hangar, or you'll be juggling power priorities mid-fight.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Multicrew CZs. Bring a friend to gun and fly with a deployed SLF — the Crusader's natural element.
  • NPC-fighter RES hunting. Even solo, a launched fighter and a high-rank NPC pilot split enemy fire and boost your effective DPS.
  • Pirate massacre stacks. Stack missions and clear them with fighter support for faster, safer sessions.
Advanced
  • Coordinated wing combat. As a crewed tank with a fighter, the Crusader anchors a wing and adds a remote gun at once.
  • Thargoid AX with a gunner. A crewed Crusader can run AX weapons while a gunner manages turrets and the fighter — though dedicated AX hulls are covered separately.
  • Teaching new pilots. The gunner and fighter seats make it a great ship for flying with a less-experienced friend.
Generic example systemsAny CZ or Haz RES suits it, best with a friend aboard. Around your Diaguandri / Ray Gateway base, crewed CZ and massacre payouts double as Imperial-standing progress.
12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Flown solo, the Crusader under-performs its siblings — its value is the crew seats and fighter bay, so use them.
  • A high-rank NPC fighter pilot dramatically increases the SLF's contribution; recruit and rank one up.
  • Put turret weapons on the small mounts so a gunner has something to control while you fly.
  • It's the slowest Alliance hull — play it as an anchor and let the fighter chase, not the mothership.
VerdictThe Alliance Crusader is the budget crewed gunboat: the Alliance tank chassis and three military slots, plus a fighter bay and three crew seats the Chieftain and Challenger lack. Solo it's the weakest of the trio, but with a deployed fighter and a gunner aboard it becomes a genuine force multiplier — the most accessible way into multicrew, SLF-supported combat on a medium pad.

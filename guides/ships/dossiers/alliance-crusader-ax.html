Series Ships Updated 2026-06-25
Briefing

The Alliance bruiser turned Xeno-hunter — three military slots of Guardian hardening on a budget medium

The Crusader brings the one stat AX rewards most: three military slots, the most of any medium, all of them taking Guardian Module Reinforcement — the hardening that keeps your modules alive through Thargoid lightning. Add the Alliance tank hull, a fighter-bay decoy and no rank gate, and you have a cheap, durable anti-Xeno platform. It sits a few points behind the Chieftain because it is the slowest Alliance medium, carries the lowest base shield, and has only four utility mounts — a real squeeze once the Xeno scanner, caustic sink and heat sink are fitted.

01

Role & Overview

Take the Alliance trio's tank chassis and count military slots: the Crusader has three — one more than the Challenger, two more than most mediums — and every one of them accepts Guardian Module Reinforcement. In anti-Xeno work that matters more than almost anything: Thargoid lightning strips and disables modules, and a stack of Guardian MRP is what keeps your thrusters, distributor and weapons online when lesser ships are flying home crippled. On top of that the Crusader keeps the tough Alliance hull, adds a ship-launched-fighter bay you can deploy as a decoy to split a swarm, and gates behind no rank at all. Arm the large mount with an AX multi-cannon, the two mediums with Guardian Gauss for heart damage, and you have a cheap, hard-to-kill Xeno hunter.

It rates 79 rather than joining the Chieftain at the top of the medium AX ladder for three honest reasons. It is the slowest Alliance medium with the lowest base shield, so it cannot weave out of a Cyclops' lightning the way a Chieftain can — and in AX, evasion is survival. It carries only four utility mounts, which a Xeno scanner, caustic sink and heat sink fill almost completely, leaving little room for the shield-booster suite. And with a single large hardpoint its raw AX firepower trails the heavier hulls. What it offers in return is module hardening and a fighter decoy no other budget medium matches.

Where this hull shines

Attritional, hardening-first AX: holding a position in an AX Conflict Zone, surviving the module-stripping lightning of Cyclops and Basilisk hunts, and any sortie where a deployed fighter splitting Thargoid attention buys the time a kill takes.

Four things make the Crusader a credible Xeno hunter:

  • Three military slots. Room for three Guardian Module Reinforcement packages — the most of any medium, and the hardening that keeps modules alive through Thargoid lightning.
  • The Alliance tank hull. 540 effective armour layered with Guardian Hull Reinforcement keeps it on station while lighter mediums break off to repair.
  • A fighter-bay decoy. Deploy an SLF to split a Thargoid's attention — a force the Chieftain and Challenger don't have.
  • No gate. No rank, no permit, ~22M Cr, medium pad — the cheapest serious AX-hardening platform a new Xeno pilot can buy.
The ceiling, stated honestly

The Crusader is the slowest Alliance medium with the lowest base shield, so it cannot dodge an Interceptor's lightning like a Chieftain — and AX rewards evasion. Its four utility mounts barely fit the mandatory Xeno scanner, caustic sink and heat sink, leaving almost no room for shield boosters. With one large hardpoint, raw AX firepower also trails the heavier hulls. Its case is module hardening and a decoy, not speed or shields.

At ~22M Cr the Crusader is one of the cheapest hulls that can field a full Guardian-hardened AX fit, and it sits behind no rank or permit — only the Guardian and AX weapon unlocks every Xeno hull needs. A complete AX fit with three Guardian MRP, Gauss and AX multi-cannons, a bi-weave shield and a fighter bay runs to roughly ~45M Cr A-rated, rising past ~65M Cr engineered, with a low ~3M rebuy that makes mistakes cheap to learn from.

That cheap rebuy and the absence of any rank gate make the Crusader a strong first dedicated AX ship: hard enough to survive the learning curve, cheap enough that a death costs little. The real price of admission is the Guardian-tech grind for the weapons and reinforcement — the same toll every AX hull pays.

Hardening on a budget

~65M Cr engineered plus the Guardian unlocks buys three military slots of module hardening on a medium pad with no rank gate — AX survivability the budget Chieftain can't quite match. Factor the Guardian-tech grind, not the credits, as the real cost.

Harden, then decoy

The Crusader's whole AX case is its three military slots — fill all three with Guardian Module Reinforcement so the lightning can't disable you. Put Gauss on the mediums for heart damage, an Enhanced AX multi-cannon on the large mount, and Guardian Hull Reinforcement across the optionals. The four utilities are tight: scanner, caustic and heat kit come first, then one shield booster. Run the SLF as a decoy when a swarm converges.

06

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy-only, the Crusader fits just its stock core internals: a 6E Power Plant, 6E Thrusters, 5E Frame Shift Drive, 5E Life Support, 6E Power Distributor, 4E Sensors and the 4C Fuel Tank. There is no rank to grind first.

Every hardpoint stays empty — the large AX multi-cannon mount, both Guardian Gauss mediums and the three Guardian-shard smalls all wait on AX and Guardian weapon unlocks that no shipyard sells.

The four utilities, all three military slots, the bi-weave shield, the fighter hangar and the Guardian Hull Reinforcement optionals sit at “—” as well, until the Guardian and AX kit is unlocked. Buy-only, she leaves the pad as a bare hull on E-rated cores — every part that makes her an AX platform comes later.

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

AX-fitting priority for the gunboat:

  • 1AX / Guardian weapons — Gauss on the mediums, AX multi-cannon on the large mount, Guardian shards on the smalls. material unlocks
  • 2Guardian reinforcement — Three Guardian MRP in the military slots plus Guardian HRP in the optionals; this is the Crusader's edge. material unlocks
  • 3Power Distributor & Plant → A — Gauss and a shield are immensely power- and capacitor-hungry. ~7M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator & booster — A bi-weave with a thermal-resistant booster over the low base shield. ~4M Cr
  • 5Thrusters → A — Recover what agility the heavy hull allows; you'll need every bit to dodge. ~4M Cr
Hardening is the case

The Crusader earns its rating by surviving the lightning, not dodging it — prioritise the three Guardian MRP and a layered shield/hull tank over chasing DPS its single large mount can't deliver anyway.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • AX Conflict Zones. Hold a position with hardened modules while the SLF splits the swarm and lighter ships dart.
  • Cyclops & Basilisk hunts. Hold Gauss on a heart; three Guardian MRP keep your modules online through the lightning.
  • Decoy tactics. Deploy the fighter to draw a Thargoid's attacks while you keep firing.
Advanced
  • Wing AX support. The hardened anchor that stays online while agile mediums dodge and harass.
  • Maelstrom & Titan sorties. Module hardening and a low rebuy make extended, dangerous runs cheap to repeat.
  • Crewed AX. A gunner can run the SLF and a turret while you focus on hearts — the Alliance crew chassis pays off in AX too.
Generic example operations

Wherever Thargoids are fought — AXCZ, Interceptor hunts, Maelstrom and Titan ops. The Crusader is the budget hull you bring when the plan is to harden up and outlast the lightning rather than dodge it.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Fill all three military slots with Guardian Module Reinforcement — module hardening is the Crusader's whole AX case.
  • Only four utilities: scanner, caustic sink and heat sink come first, leaving room for just one shield booster.
  • You can't dodge the lightning like a Chieftain — lean on hardening, the tank hull, and the fighter decoy instead.
  • Manage heat hard with Gauss, and keep the SLF ready to split a swarm; a high-rank NPC pilot makes the decoy far more useful.
Verdict

The Alliance Crusader is the budget AX hardening platform: three military slots of Guardian Module Reinforcement — the most of any medium — on a tough hull with a fighter-bay decoy and no rank gate. It scores 79, a few points behind the Chieftain and Challenger, because it is the slowest Alliance medium with the lowest base shield and only four utility mounts, so it can't dodge the lightning the way the role rewards. Buy it when you want serious module hardening cheap, and fight by outlasting the swarm rather than out-flying it.

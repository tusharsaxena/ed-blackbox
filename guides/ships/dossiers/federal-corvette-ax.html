Briefing

The combat king turned on the Thargoids — a battleship AX tank

The same 666-armour, ~516 MJ-shield warship that tops the combat ladder makes a near-unkillable anti-Xeno platform: two military slots for Guardian module hardening, eight utilities, deep internals and capital agility that holds Gauss on an Interceptor's hearts. It loses points only because no huge AX weapon exists — its signature huge mounts run oversized — and because a 900-tonne hull still can't dodge Thargoid lightning like a medium can. Behind that sits the Federal Rear Admiral grind.

01

Role & Overview

Point the best combat hull in the game at the Thargoids and you get one of the toughest AX warships flying. The Federal Corvette brings 666 battleship armour, a ~516 MJ shield, two military slots for Guardian Module Reinforcement, eight utility mounts and eleven optional internals — everything you need to soak caustic damage, ride out Interceptor lightning, and keep firing while lighter ships are already running for repair. Its surprising agility for a 900-tonne hull lets it hold Gauss cannons on an exposed heart far better than the other capitals.

The reason it sits at 86 rather than the top of the AX ladder is specific and honest: no huge AX weapon exists. The Corvette's defining pair of huge hardpoints can only mount large-or-smaller AX/Guardian guns, so its signature firepower advantage shrinks against a foe its big slots can't fully exploit. And while it turns well for a capital, it still cannot dodge a Thargoid's lightning and swarm the way a Chieftain or Krait can. It out-tanks every AX medium and matches the Type-10 for sheer durability — the Cutter just out-shields it, and the agile mediums score higher on survivable evasion.

Where this hull shines

Attritional anti-Xeno work: standing in AX Conflict Zones, anchoring a wing as the immovable damage-soak, Titan and Maelstrom operations where staying power matters, and any fight you intend to win by outlasting rather than out-dodging.

Four things make the Corvette a battleship-grade Xeno hunter:

  • Two military slots. Room for two Guardian Module Reinforcement packages — the hardening that lets it shrug off the lightning that strips lesser ships' modules.
  • Capital tank. 666 armour and a ~516 MJ shield, doubled and tripled with Guardian HRP and bi-weave, keep it on station while others retreat.
  • Eight utilities. Xeno scanner, caustic sinks, heat sinks and a full shield-booster suite all at once — no compromises on the defensive kit.
  • Capital agility plus an SLF. It turns well enough to keep guns on a heart, and the fighter bay adds a decoy to split Thargoid attention.
The ceiling, stated honestly

There is no huge AX weapon, so the Corvette's two huge hardpoints carry large-class AX/Guardian guns — powerful, but not the dominating huge mounts that win it the conventional combat crown. It also demands Federal Rear Admiral, needs a large pad, costs ~188M Cr before kit, and — for all its agility — is still too heavy to truly dodge an Interceptor's lightning. Its case is survivability and firepower-by-attrition, not evasion.

At ~188M Cr the Corvette is one of the most expensive hulls in the game, and it sits behind the Federal Rear Admiral rank — a long climb up the Dropship→FAS→Gunship ladder. A full AX fit with Guardian/AX weapons, capital shields and double-layered reinforcement runs to ~435M Cr A-rated, rising past ~480M Cr engineered, with a rebuy near ~22M to budget for.

It is an endgame AX ship, not a starter. If you've already earned the rank for a combat Corvette, converting one to AX is the natural next step — you get a Thargoid platform that almost never dies. If you're learning AX, do it in a cheaper, more forgiving Chieftain first.

Survivability costs more

~480M Cr engineered plus the Guardian-tech grind buys the toughest AX warship short of the Cutter. The Rear Admiral rank and the ~22M rebuy are the real price of admission — this is a capstone hull, flown by commanders who already own the rank.

Stand in the fire, harden everything

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

The buy-only state is a bare hull on stock core internals: an 8E Power Plant, 7E Thrusters, 6E Frame Shift Drive, 5E Life Support, 8E Power Distributor, 8E Sensors and a 5C Fuel Tank. That is the entire fit at this stage.

Every hardpoint, all eight utilities, both military slots and all eleven optional internals stay empty (—). The AX multi-cannons, Guardian Gauss and shard cannons, Xeno scanner, Guardian Module/Hull Reinforcement and the capital bi-weave shield are all unlock-gated, so none of them can be bought outright — they wait for the A-Rated pass.

Earn Rear Admiral, buy the hull, then unlock the Guardian and AX kit at the relevant sites before fitting any of it.

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

AX-fitting priority for the warship:

  • 1AX / Guardian weapons — three Enhanced AX Multi-Cannons on the huge/large mounts, two 2B Guardian Gauss Cannons on the mediums, two Guardian Shard Cannons on the smalls. material unlocks
  • 2Guardian reinforcement — two 5D Guardian MRP in the military slots plus Guardian HRP/MRP across the optionals; this is the Corvette's edge. material unlocks
  • 3Power Distributor & Plant → 8A — Gauss and a capital shield are immensely power- and capacitor-hungry. ~50M Cr
  • 4Shield Generator → 7C bi-weave & 0A boosters — A capital-scale bi-weave with thermal-resistant shield boosters against caustic and thermal damage. ~25M Cr
  • 5Thrusters → 7A, FSD → 6A — Preserve the agility that holds Gauss on a heart and the jump range to reach distant Maelstroms and Titans. ~22M Cr
Hardening is the case

The Corvette earns its rating by surviving — prioritise the two Guardian MRP and a layered shield/hull tank over chasing DPS its missing huge AX gun can't deliver anyway.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • AX Conflict Zones. Stand in the thick of an AXCZ and out-last the swarm while lighter ships cycle out.
  • Interceptor kills. Hold Gauss on a heart through Cyclops to Medusa; the tank buys the time the kill takes.
  • Decoy tactics. Deploy the SLF to split a Thargoid's attacks while you keep firing.
Advanced
  • Wing anchor. The immovable damage-soak that holds the line while agile mediums dart and harass.
  • Maelstrom & Titan operations. Staying power for the most dangerous Thargoid airspace and extended sorties.
  • Hydra and Basilisk hunts. The tank to survive the toughest Interceptor variants' sustained fire.
Generic example operations

Wherever Thargoids are fought — AXCZ, Interceptor hunts, Maelstrom and Titan ops. The Corvette is the warship you bring when the plan is to outlast the fight, not run from it.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Fill both military slots with Guardian Module Reinforcement — module hardening is the Corvette's whole AX case.
  • There is no huge AX weapon: run Gauss on the mediums and large AX multi-cannons in the huge mounts.
  • You can't dodge the lightning like a medium — layer shield and Guardian hull tank and plan to absorb it.
  • Manage heat hard with Gauss, and keep the SLF ready as a decoy when a swarm converges.
Verdict

The Federal Corvette is the battleship of anti-Xeno combat: 666 armour, a capital shield, two military slots for Guardian hardening and eight utilities make it nearly unkillable in a held AXCZ. It scores 86 — behind the Cutter's bigger shield and the agile mediums' survivable evasion — because no huge AX gun matches its signature mounts and a 900-tonne hull can't dodge the lightning. Earn the Rear Admiral rank, and you have a Thargoid warship that wins by attrition.

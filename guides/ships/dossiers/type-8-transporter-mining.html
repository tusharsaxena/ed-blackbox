01

Role & Overview

The Type-8 Transporter's huge medium-pad hold makes it a natural high-volume miner. With over 400 tonnes of capacity, it carries a full mining suite — refinery, prospector and collector controllers — and still has room for one of the biggest ore holds available on a medium pad. That means longer sessions before you have to dock, and bigger hauls of valuable ore per trip.

It's tough and reasonably quick for its size, and its medium pad reaches ring systems and stations the large miners can't. It can't fight off pirates the way a Python can, so it relies on a shield and a quick escape if interdicted — but for pure laser-mining throughput on a medium pad, nothing holds more. For a commander whose mining is about volume, the Type-8 is the capacity king of its class.

Where this hull shinesHigh-volume laser mining: long sessions in rich hotspots (platinum, painite, tritium), maximising ore per trip on a medium pad, and bulk mining to supply markets or a fleet carrier.

Four things make the Type-8 a top volume miner:

  • The biggest medium-pad ore hold. Over 400 tonnes of capacity — the most ore per trip you can land on a medium pad.
  • Room for a full suite plus huge cargo. Refinery, prospector and multiple collector controllers, and still a vast hold.
  • Tough and fairly quick. A durable modern hull with enough speed to escape interdiction better than the big freighter-miners.
  • Medium-pad and rank-free. Reaches ring systems and stations the large miners can't, with no rank gate.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIt can't fight off pirates — its hardpoints are small and it lacks the Python's guns and apex shield — so it relies on escaping interdiction, which is risky when laden with valuable ore. The capital miners hold far more. The Type-8's case is maximum medium-pad ore volume; for self-defence or sheer capacity, the Python or a capital miner respectively win.

At ~34.8M Cr the Type-8 is mid-priced — cheaper than the Python and far cheaper than the capital miners — with no rank gate. A mining fit keeps the all-in cost around 52M Cr.

It's the value high-volume miner: the most ore per trip on a medium pad, on a durable modern hull, for a fraction of a capital ship's cost. For commanders mining safe, rich hotspots, it's the smart pick.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • High-volume laser mining. Fill an enormous hold in rich hotspots before docking.
  • Bulk ore supply. Mine platinum, painite or tritium in quantity for markets or a carrier.
  • Long solo sessions. Its hold means fewer trips back to refine and sell.
Advanced
  • Tritium mining for carriers. Its capacity fuels a fleet carrier in fewer runs.
  • Medium-pad ring systems. Reaches rich rings near stations the large miners can't use.
  • Re-role to trading. The same big hold makes a fine medium freighter — it has a trading manual too.
Generic example sitesAny rich, reasonably safe metallic or icy ring suits it. From your Diaguandri / Ray Gateway base it reaches mining systems easily and brings home the biggest medium-pad ore hauls.
12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Volume is the point — fill the hold; mine richer hotspots to make the big capacity pay.
  • It can't fight pirates — keep a shield, mine safer systems, and boost away if interdicted.
  • Don't engineer the mining tools; engineer the ship's escape and survival instead.
  • For self-defence in dangerous rings, the Python is safer; for pure volume, the Type-8 wins.
VerdictThe Type-8 Transporter is the high-capacity medium miner: the biggest ore hold you can land on a medium pad, on a tough modern hull, for sensible money. It can't fight pirates like a Python, so it mines richer-but-safer hotspots and escapes trouble — but for pure ore volume short of a capital ship, nothing its size hauls more. The volume miner's choice.

01

Role & Overview

Mining is a slow, stationary business that makes you a magnet for pirates — and the Python answers that better than almost anything. Its three large hardpoints mount every mining tool you need (lasers, sub-surface missiles, a seismic charge launcher for core mining, an abrasion blaster), its deep internals carry a full refinery, limpet controllers and a big ore hold, and crucially it keeps the shields and weapons to turn and destroy the pirate that interdicts you mid-session.

On a medium pad it reaches most stations and ring systems, and its strong shields mean it survives the long, exposed minutes of cracking a motherlode. It carries less ore than the capital miners or a stripped Type-8, and it costs more — but no medium miner combines this much capability with genuine self-defence. For mining valuable cores in pirate-prone rings, the Python is the safe, capable, do-everything choice.

Where this hull shinesDefensible, capable mining: core and laser mining in pirate-prone rings, painite/platinum/tritium hotspots, and any session where surviving the pirates who hunt laden miners matters as much as the haul.

Four things make the Python a superb miner:

  • Mounts every mining tool. Three large hardpoints fit lasers, sub-surface missiles and a seismic charge launcher for laser and core mining at once.
  • Deep internals for a full suite. Refinery, prospector and multiple collector controllers, a detailed surface scanner and a big ore hold all fit together.
  • It defends itself. Strong shields and the option to arm a gun mean it destroys the pirates that interdict laden miners, rather than fleeing.
  • Medium-pad and rank-free. Reaches most stations and ring systems; anyone with the credits can fly one.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIts ore hold trails the capital miners (Cutter, Type-9, Anaconda) and even a stripped Type-8, so for sheer tonnage per trip it isn't the leader, and at ~55M Cr it's pricier than the dedicated trade-miners. The Python's case is capability plus self-defence on a medium pad; for maximum raw haul or lowest cost, other miners win.

At ~55.3M Cr the Python is pricier than the trade-miners, with no rank gate. A mining fit — tools, refinery, limpet controllers, ore racks and a defensible shield — brings the all-in figure to around 88M Cr.

Its versatility offsets the cost: the same hull mines, trades, fights or explores. For pure tonnage a Type-8 or capital miner is cheaper per tonne; for a capable miner that also defends itself and re-roles to anything, the Python earns its price.

Capability and safetyAround 88M Cr all-in for a miner that fights off pirates and re-roles to anything — you pay for capability and survival, not raw tonnage.

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull and fit the mining suite: lasers, a seismic launcher, refinery and limpet controllers.

Keep one large mount for a gun and fit a strong bi-weave shield for self-defence.

Fill remaining optionals with ore racks; A-rate the plant and distributor to run tools and guns.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Core mining in pirate rings. Crack high-value cores (painite, void opals, low-temperature diamonds) and survive the attention.
  • Laser mining hotspots. Work platinum, tritium or painite hotspots with a full collector swarm.
  • Mixed mine-and-defend. Mine until interdicted, then turn and destroy the pirate.
Advanced
  • Solo deep-ring expeditions. Its self-defence means no escort needed in dangerous systems.
  • Re-role on demand. The same hull mines, trades, fights or explores.
  • Tritium mining for carriers. Mine fuel for a fleet carrier and defend the haul.
Generic example sitesAny pristine metallic or icy ring suits it. From your Diaguandri / Ray Gateway base it reaches mining systems easily and works them without fear of pirates — mine, refine, and defend the ore home.
12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Keep a gun and a strong shield — the Python's edge is surviving the pirates that hunt laden miners.
  • Don't engineer the mining tools — they don't benefit; engineer the ship's defence and mobility instead.
  • A-rate the distributor and plant so lasers, limpets and a gun all run together.
  • For pure tonnage a Type-8 or capital miner holds more; choose the Python for safety and versatility.
VerdictThe Python is the miner that defends itself: every mining tool, a full limpet-and-refinery suite, a big ore hold and a medium pad — plus the shields and guns to destroy the pirates that prey on laden miners. The capital ships haul more ore, but none mines as safely or flexibly. For working valuable cores in dangerous rings without an escort, the Python is the standout medium choice.

Role & Overview

The Eagle Mk II is Core Dynamics' featherweight interceptor and the most agile production ship in its class. Three small hardpoints and a turn rate that embarrasses almost everything make it a pure dogfighter — it sits on a target's tail and never lets go. At around 11,000 credits it's also one of the cheapest ships in the game.

The price of that agility is everything else. Its hull is an eggshell, its shields minimal, it has just one utility mount and one small military slot, and its three small guns deal little damage. The Eagle is unforgiving: it wins by never being hit and by out-turning opponents into the ground, but a single solid salvo can end it. It's a thrill and a superb teacher of dogfighting, but a glass dart in every sense.

Where this hull shinesPure dogfighting practice and budget thrills: learning to turn-fight and hold position on a target, low-stakes RES, and cheap, agile fun where being un-hittable matters more than hitting hard.

Four things define the Eagle as a combat ship:

  • Best-in-class agility. It out-turns almost everything — the core of its dogfighting identity, letting it stay on a target that can't follow.
  • Three small hardpoints. A surprising three guns for so tiny a hull — modest damage, but more mounts than many expect.
  • A military slot. One class-2 bay lets it harden its fragile hull a fraction — every point counts here.
  • Almost free. At ~11k credits with a trivial rebuy, it's the cheapest path to a dedicated dogfighter.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIt is extraordinarily fragile — the thinnest hull and shields in the class, one utility, a class-2 plant and three small guns. It can't tank a single serious salvo, deals little damage, and a moment's mistake is fatal. The Eagle's only virtue is agility; everything else is sacrificed. It's a teacher and a toy, not a platform you keep.

At ~11k Cr the Eagle is among the cheapest ships in the game, with no rank gate and a trivial rebuy. Even fully kitted it stays inexpensive, though engineering a hull this fragile yields limited returns.

It's a pure value-and-learning pick: a near-free dogfighter to master agility in, or a cheap thrill to fly for its own sake. Few pilots keep one long, but many remember theirs fondly.

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull (~11k Cr) and fit a shield plus the single military-slot reinforcement.

Arm the three smalls — multi-cannons for damage, one Corrosive once you have Tod McQuinn.

Fly to learn the turn fight; stay on tails, never trade blows head-on.

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

A-rating priority for a dogfighter:

  • 1Thrusters → A — Agility is the Eagle's whole identity; A-rate first. ~250k Cr
  • 2Power Distributor → A — Sustain three guns and boost on a tiny plant. ~150k Cr
  • 3Power Plant → A — Headroom for guns, shield and the booster. ~150k Cr
  • 4Shield Generator → bi-weave — The thin shield needs to regen fast. ~120k Cr
  • 5FSD → A — Reach RES sites and turn-ins. ~150k Cr
Thrusters above allOn the Eagle, agility is survival — A-rate and engineer thrusters before anything; staying on the enemy's blind side is the only defence the hull has.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Learning to dogfight. The Eagle is the purest teacher of the turn fight — staying on tails and holding guns on.
  • Low-stakes RES. Pick off the weakest bounties while practising agility at near-zero risk.
  • Cheap thrills. Fly it for the sheer joy of its handling, rebuy be damned (it's tiny).
Advanced
  • Agility mastery. Skills learned wringing performance from the fragile Eagle transfer to every more capable ship.
  • Budget aggression. Its near-zero rebuy lets you fly recklessly and learn from mistakes cheaply.
  • Wing fighter screen. As a nimble, expendable harasser it can chase and pin targets for tougher wingmates.
Generic example systemsAny low RES near an outpost suits it — keep fights small and winnable. Around your Diaguandri / Ray Gateway home it's a near-free way to practise dogfighting.
12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Thrusters and agility are everything — the Eagle has no other defence; stay where enemy guns can't follow.
  • Never trade blows head-on; one solid salvo ends the fight.
  • It's a teacher and a toy — master it, then carry the agility skills to a Vulture or Fer-de-Lance.
  • Its sibling the Imperial Eagle is slightly faster and tougher with a medium gun if you want a touch more substance.
VerdictThe Eagle Mk II is agility distilled: the most nimble fighter in its class, three small guns, and a price of almost nothing — wrapped around an eggshell that one mistake will shatter. It's no platform to keep, but as a pure dogfighter, a teacher of the turn fight, and a cheap thrill, few ships are more instructive or more fun.

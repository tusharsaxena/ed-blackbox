Unlock Checklist Reference Manual

Series Engineering Updated 2026-07-01
Field Briefing

Engineering is a referral tree, run as one trip.

You begin with five Tier 1 ship engineers who each ask for one simple condition and one hand-in. Climbing any of them to Grade 3 (and about one-third into Grade 4) triggers referrals to Tier 2, and Tier 2 referrals open Tier 3 — where permits and rank grinds gate the deepest engineers. This manual flattens that tree into a single chronological run of discrete steps: prep and farms first, then each tier in dependency order, the permit/rank grinds, the Colonia expedition, and finally the on-foot Odyssey engineers. Every step names its action type and exactly what to do. Material quantities live in the Engineering Tracker; the routes for each material live in the four farm guides linked from Phase 0.

Legend

Every step is one card. The left rail carries a running step number and the card's coloured left edge marks its action type. Engineer names link to the Engineers manual; module and blueprint names link to the Blueprints manual; farm steps link to the matching field guide. Each step (and each phase header) has a permanent # anchor for deep-linking.

ClimbRoll blueprints to earn a referral
BuildTech-broker module (no blueprint)

The referral mechanic

A Tier 1/2 engineer refers you onward once you reach Grade 3 plus ~⅓ of the way into Grade 4 with them.
That's roughly 4–5 rolls past Grade 3 — not a full Grade 4.
It counts across all their blueprints, so roll whatever is cheapest or whatever you actually want.

03

Phase 0 · Prep & Farms

Setup

Before chasing engineers, set the ship up for surface work and stand up the four material farms. You do not have to cap everything now — but every "climb a blueprint for the referral" step downstream spends materials, so having the farm routes bookmarked saves a lot of backtracking. The farm guides carry the exact sites, coordinates and loops; the Engineering Tracker carries the quantities.

In this phase · 1 setup + 4 farm steps · supports every later climb
STEP01
Prep

Fit a Planetary Vehicle Hangar (SRV) and keep some cargo racks free

#
Where
Any station with an Outfitting service
Fit
Planetary Vehicle Hangar (size 2 is plenty) + a Detailed Surface Scanner
Keep free
A few empty cargo racks for hand-in commodities
What to do: dock at any station, open Outfitting, and buy a Planetary Vehicle Hangar — fit it into a spare optional-internal slot. That gives you an SRV (the surface buggy) you'll drive around the material farms to scoop materials off the ground. Also fit a Detailed Surface Scanner in a utility slot; you use it to "map" a planet and reveal exactly where each farm site sits. Finally, leave two or three cargo racks empty — several engineers are unlocked by handing over a tradeable commodity (Meta Alloy, Soontill Relics, Painite, Landmines…) that you must physically carry in your hold. A fuel-scooping exploration ship like an Asp doubles perfectly as your farm ship.

What to do: this site covers the encoded third of the materials grid. 04

Phase 1 · Tier 1 Engineers

Ship · 5

The entry tier — no referral or permit gates. Meet a simple condition, hand in one item, and you're in. Each of these five is followed by a Climb step: rolling them to Grade 3 (a little into Grade 4) is what unlocks the Tier 2 engineers they refer you to. Two of them (Martuuk, Liz Ryder) are mainly gateways — you climb them only for the referral.

Unlocks · Felicity Farseer · Elvira Martuuk · The Dweller · Tod McQuinn · Liz Ryder

Exploration rank Scout or higher

What to do: first, reach exploration rank Scout — if you're not there yet, fire your Discovery Scanner (the "honk") as you arrive in a few systems and sell the exploration data at any station's Universal Cartographics; a handful of systems is enough. Next, get one Meta Alloy and carry it in a cargo rack. Then fly to Deciat, request docking at Farseer Inc on body 6 A, land, open the engineer's contact panel and hand the Meta Alloy in. Farseer is the single most useful first unlock — pin FSD Increased Range straight away so you can re-roll it remotely, and the whole rest of this run (long jumps everywhere) gets easier.
NoteFarseer moved to Deciat during the Thargoid War (older guides still say Maia). The Meta Alloy is still sold at Darnielle's Progress in Maia (~80k CR), but you can now also scoop one free from any Titan wreck debris field, or buy one from a fleet carrier parked in Deciat.

Phase 3 · Permits & Ranks

Grind

The deepest engineers sit behind faction ranks and system permits. None of this is a single sit-down grind — most of it accrues from the combat, trade and travel you’re already doing. The key distinction: Federal and Imperial rank permits are granted automatically on rank-up, while the Alioth permit needs a specific mission after you max Alliance reputation.

Gates · Federal Navy · Imperial Navy · Alioth permit · Elite → Shinrarta · Combat / Trade / Exploration ranks
STEP27
Grind

Rank up the Federal Navy → Sol permit & Midshipman

#
Gates
The Sarge (Midshipman) · Colonel Bris Dekker (Sol permit + Friendly)
How
Federal Navy missions → rank-up; permit auto-granted
What to do: Run missions for Federation-aligned factions to raise your Federal Navy rank; “Navy promotion” (Ascension) missions appear once you’re close to the next rank. The Sarge needs rank Midshipman; the Sol permit (for Bris Dekker) is granted automatically at the required Federal rank — no separate permit mission. Sothis/Ceos courier missions are a fast rank route.
STEP28

#
Gates
Hera Tani (Outsider) · Tiana Fortune (Achenar permit + Friendly)
How
Imperial missions → rank-up; permit auto-granted
What to do: Run missions for Empire-aligned factions. Outsider (for Hera Tani) is the first Imperial rank and comes almost instantly. Keep going for the Achenar permit (for Tiana Fortune), which is granted automatically on reaching the required Imperial rank. Imperial donation/charity missions (e.g. around Ngalinn/Aitvas) rank you fast.
STEP29
Grind

Earn the Alioth permit → Alliance reputation + permit mission

#
Gates
Bill Turner (Alliance Friendly + Alioth permit)
How
Max rep with Alioth Independents, then a permit mission
What to do: Reach Friendly with the Alliance and max reputation (Allied) with the Alioth Independents faction by running their missions. Unlike Federal/Imperial permits, the Alioth permit is not auto-granted — once you’re Allied, head to 78 Ursae Majoris / Rominger Dock and accept the permit-grant mission from the Alioth Independents representatives.
STEP30
Grind

#
Gates
Lori Jameson (Shinrarta Dezhra permit)
How
Hit Elite I in any one career
What to do: Reach rank Elite (grade I) in any single career — Combat, Trade or Exploration. Doing so grants the Shinrarta Dezhra (Founders World) permit automatically, which is where Lori Jameson lives. Exploration tends to be the quickest career to Elite via long data-selling expeditions.
STEP31

Push your Combat rank → Competent → Dangerous → Expert

#
Gates
Broo Tarquin (Competent) · Lori Jameson (Dangerous) · Petra Olmanova (Expert)

Bounty hunting & Combat Zones
What to do: These three combat-rank gates come naturally from the bounty hunting and Combat-Zone work you already do for Tod McQuinn and Juri Ishmaak. Keep ratting RES sites and CZs until you’re at least Expert and all three are covered.
STEP32

What to do: Low-stakes ranks that accrue passively. Merchant (Didi) and Dealer (Etienne) come from trade profit — a few bulk hauls do it. Surveyor (Ram Tah, Marsha Hicks) comes from selling exploration data, which you’ll have piles of from the permit-and-Colonia travel.

Phase 5 · Colonia Expedition

22 kly · 8

Eight engineers — four ship, four on-foot — clustered ~22,000 ly away in the Colonia region. Treat it as one expedition: prep, fly out, and clear all eight before heading back (or relocate a carrier). Selling exploration data on the way out does most of the Colonia Council rep work for you.

Unlocks · Petra Olmanova · Mel Brandon · Etienne Dorn · Marsha Hicks · Baltanos · Eleanor Bresa · Rosa Dayette · Yi Shen
STEP44
Prep

Stage the Colonia unlock cargo & materials

#
Fit
High jump range + big fuel scoop + SRV + limpet controllers
Bring
Engineering mats to roll there; optional pre-mined Osmium
Cargo
Hatch Breaker + Collector limpets + ≥25 t free (for Escape Pods)
What to do: The four ship engineers here are ~22,000 ly away, so do them in one expedition. Outfit a long-range, big-scoop ship with an SRV. Most unlock goods (Progenitor Cells, Occupied Escape Pods, on-foot data) can be sourced in Colonia, but bring the engineering materials you’ll want to actually roll modules there (Colonia outfitting is limited), and consider carrying 10 t of mined Osmium for Marsha Hicks so you don’t have to mine on arrival. For Etienne Dorn you’ll need a Hatch Breaker + Collector limpet setup and ≥25 t of free cargo.

That single act satisfies Baltanos’s Friendly requirement and triggers Mel Brandon’s Engineer Invitation Contract mission (deliver data to Whirling Station in Ogmar).

Phase 6 · Bubble Odyssey

On-foot · 9

Nine on-foot (suit & weapon) engineers back in the bubble, reached by selling data/goods to concourse bartenders and running on-foot missions. Three are common-knowledge entry points; the rest come by referral. Since the Aug 2024 buff, the unlock items drop as mission rewards, so this is far lighter than it used to be — take missions that pay the items you need.

Unlocks · Domino Green · Hero Ferrari · Jude Navarro · Kit Fowler · Wellington Beck · Terra Velasquez · Oden Geiger · Yarden Bond · Uma Laszlo

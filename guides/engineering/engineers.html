Engineering Manuals // Engineers

Engineers Reference Manual

Series Engineering Updated 2026-06-25
Briefing

Engineering is a referral tree, not a shop

You start with a handful of Tier 1 engineers who ask only that you meet a simple condition and hand in one item. Climbing each of them to grade 3–4 unlocks referrals to Tier 2, and Tier 2 referrals open Tier 3; permits and rank grinds gate the deepest tier. There are 38 engineers in all — 25 ship-side modifying hulls and modules, and 13 Odyssey engineers modifying suits and handheld weapons. This manual catalogues every one, grouped by tier, with the meeting requirement, unlock hand-in, referral chain and full blueprint list for each.

01

What the Engineers Are

Primer

The Engineers are secretive, eccentric specialists who modify your ship's weapons and modules — and, in Odyssey, your suits and handheld weapons — to exceed their baseline performance. Each works out of a Workshop on a planetary surface (you need a Planetary Approach Suite to land); turn up without an invitation and the site treats you as trespassing and opens fire.

Engineering is how a stock ship becomes a capable one — a longer-jumping FSD, harder-hitting weapons, tougher armour, cooler-running power. You don't buy a finished upgrade: you spend materials (scavenged from wrecks, missions, scans, mining and planetary sites) to roll a blueprint onto a module at a chosen grade. Every engineer offers their own blueprints and is unlocked and ranked separately.

How an engineer works

  • Unlock — meet the invite requirement and hand in the unlock item to gain access. The five Tier-1 ship engineers need only a simple condition; Tier 2 and Tier 3 are reached by referrals from engineers you've already climbed — the tree this manual maps.
  • Reputation, grades 1–5 — each ship engineer levels through five grades as you craft with them, and the stronger rolls unlock at the higher grades. Buying modifications builds reputation; some engineers also take commodities, exploration data or bounty vouchers.
  • Roll a blueprint — spend materials to apply a modification. A roll's downside is fixed for its grade; the upside improves the more materials you invest.
  • Experimental effects — at the Workshop you can spend extra materials to add one experimental effect on top of a modification for a further edge.
  • Pin for remote use — pin one blueprint per engineer and re-roll it later at any station with a Remote Workshop, no return trip needed.
  • Odyssey is different — the 13 on-foot engineers modify suits and handheld weapons and grant their mods immediately on meeting the requirement — no grade grind.
Unlock them in the right order

Because Tier 2/3 access is gated behind referrals, permits and rank, the order you tackle engineers in matters. The Unlock Run checklist sequences all 38 into one dependency-correct trip — the ideal way to unlock the whole roster without backtracking. This manual is the reference for who's who; the Unlock Run is the route.

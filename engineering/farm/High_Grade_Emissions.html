01What an HGE Is

A High Grade Emission is a transient signal source floating in supercruise. Drop into one and it's empty except for free-floating canisters of a single Grade-5 manufactured material. Which material depends entirely on the controlling faction's allegiance and state — covered in §05.

Since the July/August 2024 engineering rework, drop rates are enormous: every canister scooped is worth 3 units, and a single signal usually holds enough to fill that G5 from zero to the cap of 100 in one or two visits. Anything written before mid-2024 badly understates how fast this now is.

Why bother, when you only farm a few? Engineering eats G5 mats fast across a fleet, and the material trader lets you convert one abundant mat into the rare ones. Cap the easy stuff once, trade for the rest — see the checklist in §06.

02Outfitting

HGEs are empty and non-hostile, so no weapons or defences are needed. Any ship that mounts a collector limpet controller and carries limpets works. Of the current fleet the Asp Explorer is the natural pick — strong jump range for hopping between systems and plenty of optional slots. Strip the exploration internals and run a throwaway farming variant:

Collector Limpet Controller1–2× (3A / 5A) — two = double scoop speed
Cargo Rack1–2× (e.g. 4E + 2E ≈ 20t)
Limpets (cargo)~40, bought at any station market
FSDKeep engineered — range = faster system hops
Drop / not neededWeapons, SRV, AFMU, shields optional

Refinery not required. Cargo capacity only ever needs to hold limpets — never the loot (see below).

03Cargo Racks & Collector Limpets

This is the part that confuses people, so here's the mechanism end to end:

Limpets are cargo

A limpet is a 1-ton consumable drone bought at a station's commodity market. It sits in your hold until launched. That is the only reason a farming ship needs cargo racks — to carry the limpets themselves. 40 limpets = 40 tons of space.

The Collector Limpet Controller launches them

Sitting inside an HGE surrounded by floating canisters, you deploy limpets and each one flies out on its own, grabs a canister, returns it to your ship, and repeats — no manual fly-and-scoop. A controller can run several limpets at once depending on rating, so two controllers roughly doubles throughput.

The loot never touches your hold

Manufactured materials go straight into your weightless materials inventory (the backpack that follows you between ships), not your cargo hold. That's why cargo size only matters for limpets. You'll know a material is capped at 100 when the limpets simply stop collecting it.

04The Relog Farming Loop

Find one good HGE, drain it, then re-spawn the same signal by relogging instead of waiting for RNG. With current drop rates you'll often cap in one or two drops without even needing the relog.

  1. Jump into a target system. Open the left Nav panel and filter it to Signal Sources only.
  2. Find a High Grade Emission. Select it and check the timer top-right — aim for 25+ minutes. A shorter one still works; you just won't max out in a single session.
  3. Drop in. On the contacts/cargo panel, confirm the canisters are the material you want before committing.
  4. Deploy all limpets and let them vacuum the instance clean.
  5. The instant you see "no valid collection targets," the instance is empty.
  6. Relog: quit to desktop (or main menu), log back into the same game mode (Solo is fastest).
  7. Back in supercruise, set throttle to zero — the same HGE is right behind you. Turn around, use Supercruise Assist to drop back in.
  8. Repeat from step 4 until limpets refuse to collect (material capped at 100).
  9. Fly to a Manufactured Material Trader and cross-trade for the rare mats.
Starter spot: Achenar (Empire, high-pop) is the textbook Imperial Shielding farm — cap shielding there, then trade it down and across for nearly everything else.

07Finding Live HGEs

  • HGE Finder — edgalaxy.net/hge — the current go-to. Lists HGE detections reported by commanders since the last BGS tick, sorted by the G5 material you want. (Replaces the defunct CMDR's Toolbox finder in your apps directory; it occasionally goes down.)
  • In-game Galaxy Map filters — most reliable fallback. Filter by allegiance + state from §05, favour high-population systems, jump in, check the Nav panel.
  • EDCoPilot — on system entry it announces which rare manufactured mats that system's HGEs can produce. Handy while hopping.
  • Inara → Nearest Stations (Manufactured Material Trader filter) — locate where to cross-trade once you're loaded up.
If a system shows no signals: jump out to a neighbour and back to reset the instance, or move to the next same-state system — waiting in place for a respawn is slower than hopping.

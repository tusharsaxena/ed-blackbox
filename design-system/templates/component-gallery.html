Card grid — column count (.four / .three / .two / .one)

The count modifier on a .cards grid names how many cards share one content-width row, applied to the whole container (a section, not a single card): .four (~4/row, terse ≤75-word cards); .three (3/row); .two (75–150-word cards, half content width, 2/row); .one (150+ word prose cards, full content width, 1/row). Pick by the largest card — longer cards want fewer, wider columns — and apply one count to the whole row so it stays even. Counts collapse responsively (.four/.three → 2-up on tablet, all → 1-up on phone). The titled-card .c-eyebrow sub-label always sits on its own line under the title, left-aligned. scripts/classify-card-groups.mjs sets plain groups to their card count and is re-runnable. Accent: cards are amber on every page; recolor one with .ac-amber/.ac-fed/.ac-maroon/.ac-good, or make a card (or the whole .cards container) follow the page accent with class="accent-page".

Systems & factions

The board

A content-heavy card in the 75–150-word band. The half-width .cards.two grid gives longer cards room to breathe instead of stacking them into a thin, hard-to-read column. Use it for a whole section when its biggest card lands in this range — the rest of the section's cards ride along at the same width so the row stays even. The eyebrow below the title is the secondary label for the card.

Influence

The score

Second wide card. The eyebrow (THE SCORE) renders on line two, left-aligned beneath the title — never floated to the right where it would collide with a wrapping title. Two cards share the content width on a normal-width viewport and collapse to one column on narrow screens, so nothing is ever squeezed below a readable measure on a phone.

The full-width case

one

When a section's largest card runs past ~150 words, the half-width grid still pinches it, so .cards.one drops to a single full-content-width column — roughly four times the width of a .cards.four card. Reach for it on the genuinely long, prose-heavy explainer cards: a paragraph or two of dense reference text that the reader works through linearly rather than scanning across a row of peers. Because you pick the count by the largest card in the group and apply it to the whole container, every card in the section adopts the same width, so a single long card promotes its neighbours to full width too rather than leaving a ragged, mixed-width row. Keep terse cards at .cards.four; reserve this widest tier for the cards that truly need the horizontal room to stay legible.

Step cards — richer task cards

For a long, action-typed run (an engineer-unlock checklist, a multi-phase build), use .step-cards > article.step-card.ac-<type>. A numbered rail + action badge, an action heading, a row of labelled fields (.rc → amber .rcl label + .rcv value), and a "what to do" block. Accent the rail/badge with ac-prep|farm|travel|grind|unlock|climb|build.

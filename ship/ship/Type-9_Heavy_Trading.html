01

Role & Overview

The Type-9 Heavy is Lakon's dedicated bulk freighter and, for years, the credits-only answer to 'how do I move the most cargo?'. Eleven optional internals — topped by two class-8 slots — let it pack close to 790 tonnes when fully racked, a figure only the Imperial Cutter and the newer Panther Clipper Mk II exceed.

It earns its keep on volume, not finesse. The hull is slow (132 m/s base), turns like a moon, and carries almost no shield or weaponry in a trading fit. The trade-off is access and economy: no rank gate, a hull cost well under the Cutter's, and the lowest effective cost-per-tonne-hauled of any easily-obtained ship. The community treats it as the workhorse you graduate to from a Type-6/Type-7 and only graduate past if you chase a Cutter or Panther.

Where this hull shinesHigh-volume trade loops, wing-mission cargo stacking, tritium runs for fleet carriers, and Trailblazers colonisation hauling. Anywhere the job is 'move as much as possible per trip' on routes safe enough that you don't need to fight.

Three things make the Type-9 a dedicated trader rather than a multirole that happens to carry cargo:

  • Two class-8 optional slots. A pair of 256-tonne cargo racks is the foundation of its ~790-tonne ceiling — no other credits-only hull has this much rackable volume.
  • Eleven optional internals. Even after fitting a shield, a Guardian FSD booster and a fuel scoop you're still carrying the bulk — the slot count absorbs the survival kit without gutting capacity.
  • A genuinely tough hull. 864 base armour means that even with a light shield it shrugs off the occasional interdiction long enough to high-wake out.
  • No rank gate, low hull cost. Unlike the Cutter (Imperial Duke) it's a straight credits purchase, so it's the realistic bulk hauler for most commanders.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIt is slow and it cannot fight. A trading Type-9 boosts to ~200 m/s and turns ponderously, so a determined PvP interdictor or a hostile NPC wing is a real threat — your defence is a shield, a high-wake FSD and route selection, not guns. The Cutter hauls nearly as much and tanks/runs far better; the Panther Clipper out-carries it outright.

The Type-9 is a straight credits purchase — no rank, permit or engineer gate — at most shipyards that stock large ships. At ~72M Cr for the hull it's a serious investment, but it's a fraction of the Imperial Cutter's ~208M and asks for no Imperial rank grind, which is exactly why it's the realistic bulk hauler for most commanders.

The trading fit is cheap relative to the hull: cargo racks, a Guardian FSD booster and a light shield. Use Inara's commodity and route tools to find loops that pay back the hull quickly — a well-chosen loop clears the purchase in a handful of runs.

Budget realityHull ~72M; a full trading fit of class-8 cargo racks plus a light shield and Guardian booster adds roughly 10–20M Cr. The single most valuable add is the Guardian FSD Booster — it costs materials, not credits, and buys back range you spend on cargo.

ARX Pre-Built Option

Frontier sells a Type-9 Trading Jumpstart on the ARX store: the hull plus a worked, lightly pre-engineered trading loadout aimed at getting you hauling immediately. It's a fast on-ramp, but the usual pre-built caveats apply — the stock modules can't be stored or transferred and sell for 0 Cr.

Treat the Jumpstart as instant access to the role rather than a permanent fit: keep and engineer in place the A-rated cores (engineering a stock core does not void the free rebuy), and simply add cargo racks to the open optional slots.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Goal: start hauling bulk immediately for the least extra spend. Fill the two class-8 and the class-7 slots with cargo racks — that's ~640 tonnes before you do anything else. Add a 6A Frame Shift Drive so a laden run still jumps usefully, a light bi-weave shield so an interdiction doesn't instantly kill you, and a Guardian FSD Booster to claw back the range the cargo costs you.

Keep thrusters and power plant D-rated and light — you're not dogfighting, you're surviving long enough to high-wake. One shield booster and a heat sink round it out. This buy-only bulk fit adds roughly 10–15M Cr over the hull and immediately moves serious tonnage.

08

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

To reach the best buy-only bulk-and-range fit before engineering, upgrade in this order:

  • 1Fill every cargo slot. Class-8s first, then work down — capacity is the entire point of the hull.
  • 2FSD to 6A + Guardian FSD Booster. Range is what cargo costs you; buy it back here. The Guardian booster is materials, not credits.
  • 3Light bi-weave shield (6D). Cheap to power, regenerates fast, and turns a fatal interdiction into a survivable one.
  • 4Power Plant to 6A, lightweight LS & sensors. Power headroom for the shield and booster, and weight off for range.
  • 5Point defence + shield booster. Soft defences against missiles and a little extra shield for the run home.
Total A-rated costRoughly 12–20M Cr on top of the hull for a full bulk-and-range fit. The Guardian FSD Booster is the highest-impact item and costs materials — unlock it before you commit to long routes.

Recommended order

  • 1FSD — Increased Range + Mass Manager (Farseer). The one roll that matters; it directly raises laden jump and shrinks your route times.
  • 2Lightweight the cores — LS, Sensors (Farseer). Lower optimal mass means more range per laden jump.
  • 3Power Plant — Low Emissions or Armoured (Hera Tani). Cool, efficient power for the shield and booster; Armoured if you want extra module durability.
  • 4Shield — Lo-Draw (Lei Cheung). Keeps the light shield cheap to run so you can spend power on the FSD/booster.
Material intensity (qualitative)Light — heavy on encoded for the FSD, a little raw for thrusters and lightweight rolls. Felicity Farseer (maxed in your fleet) does the bulk of the work in one session; no combat materials needed.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Single-commodity trade loops between two nearby stations with a strong buy/sell spread — the simplest reliable income; fill, fly, sell, repeat.
  • Stacked delivery / wing cargo missions from a busy hub — take several haulage missions for the same destination and clear them in one massive load.
  • Where: use Inara's commodity-and-route tools to find profitable loops near your base — high-population systems with Industrial/Refinery economies are the classic trade pairs.
Advanced
  • Tritium hauling to refuel and reposition fleet carriers — the Type-9's volume makes it the default carrier tanker.
  • Trailblazers colonisation supply — hauling construction commodities to colony depots pays well and consumes bulk volume exactly the way the Type-9 wants.
  • Rare-goods circuits for high per-tonne margins on longer routes, and bulk Powerplay commodity hauling for merits.
  • Where: Spansh and Inara for live commodity prices and carrier tritium needs; colonisation depots in frontier systems for construction hauling.
Safety noteA trading Type-9 is nearly defenceless. Fly laden through high-security space where possible, submit-then-high-wake out of interdictions rather than fighting, and keep the FSD spun up. Your survival kit is a shield, a fast FSD and route choice — never guns.
12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • It cannot fight — don't try. A trading Type-9 has no meaningful weapons or agility. Against interdiction, submit, boost away and high-wake; against PvP gankers, prefer Solo or a Private Group for valuable runs.
  • Large pad only. It can't dock at outposts or many planetary ports — if a route ends at a medium pad, that's the Type-8's job, not the Type-9's.
  • The hull is genuinely tough. 864 armour means even a light-shield fit can eat a few hits while you spin up the FSD — lean on that, not on dogfighting.
  • Mass-lock matters. Its high mass-lock makes it hard for small ships to mass-lock you into staying — once your FSD is charged, you leave.
  • It also mines. The same huge hold makes a strong laser-mining platform — see the Type-9 Heavy Mining dossier for that configuration.
VerdictThe Type-9 Heavy is the credits-only bulk-trading benchmark: ~720 tonnes of cargo at a sensible laden range, on a tough hull that needs no rank to fly. 94/100 — out-carried only by the Panther Clipper Mk II and matched-but-out-survived only by the rank-locked Imperial Cutter. If your job is moving the most goods per trip for the least credits-per-tonne, this is the workhorse — fly it laden, fly it cautious, and let the volume do the earning.

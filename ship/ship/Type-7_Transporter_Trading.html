Role & Overview

The Type-7 Transporter is Lakon's mid-sized freighter: a cheap, no-nonsense large-pad hauler that carries ~310 tonnes and jumps well laden, for under 17M credits. It's the sensible bridge between medium traders like the Type-6 and the expensive big freighters — enough capacity to earn serious money, at a price a growing trader can actually afford.

Its good laden jump range is a real plus, making longer, more profitable routes efficient. But it's lightly defended, slow, and — oddly for its size — large-pad-only despite carrying less than some medium ships, so it can't use outposts. The Type-7 is a workhorse, not a thoroughbred: buy it to scale up your trading income affordably, then graduate to a Type-9, Cutter or Panther when the credits allow.

Where this hull shinesAffordable scaling-up trading: medium-to-large commodity runs, longer profitable routes (thanks to good laden range), and the natural next freighter for a trader outgrowing medium haulers.

Four things make the Type-7 a sensible mid-tier trader:

  • Solid cargo for the price. ~310 tonnes — a real step up from medium haulers — on a hull under 17M credits.
  • Good laden jump range. Better than most freighters, making longer, more profitable routes efficient.
  • Cheap and rank-free. An affordable large-pad freighter anyone can buy — the accessible scaling-up trader.
  • Ten optionals. Deep enough to carry a big load plus a defensive shield without much compromise.
The ceiling, stated honestlyIt's lightly defended, slow, and large-pad-only despite carrying less than some medium ships — an awkward combination that blocks outpost trading. The Type-8 (medium pad) carries more and lands more flexibly. The Type-7's case is a cheap large hold with good range; the premium haulers and the medium Type-8 each beat it in their own way.

At ~16.8M Cr the Type-7 is cheap for a large-pad freighter, with no rank gate. A trade fit needs little engineering beyond thrusters, FSD and a shield, keeping the all-in cost around 30M Cr.

It's an affordable scaling-up pick: buy it to grow your trading income before committing to a Type-9, Cutter or Panther. Just weigh it honestly against the medium Type-8, which often makes more sense.

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Buy the hull (~16.8M Cr) and fill the optionals with cargo racks plus one bi-weave shield.

A-rate thrusters and FSD so the laden ship moves and jumps well.

Add shield boosters and point defence on the utilities for basic protection.

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Exploit the laden range — the Type-7's strength is efficient long routes.
  • Keep a bi-weave shield; the hull is lightly built and pirates notice big cargo.
  • Weigh it honestly against the medium Type-8, which carries more and lands more flexibly.
  • It's a stepping-stone — graduate to a Type-9, Cutter or Panther as capital grows.
VerdictThe Type-7 Transporter is the mid-tier workhorse: a cheap large-pad freighter with solid cargo and a good laden range, the sensible bridge from medium haulers to the big freighters. Its light defences and odd large-pad-only footprint (carrying less than some mediums) hold it back — but for affordably scaling up trading income, it does an honest job.

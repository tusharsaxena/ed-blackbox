01

Role & Overview

The Asp Explorer is Lakon's purpose-built deep-space hull and, for the better part of a decade, the community's default recommendation for a first serious explorer. Its signature is the cockpit — an enormous wrap-around canopy that makes scanning, fuel-scooping and just looking at the galaxy a pleasure no other ship in its bracket matches.

Functionally it does everything an expedition needs on a medium pad: a class-5 FSD that engineers into the 60-plus light-year range with a Guardian booster, enough optional internals for a fuel scoop, AFMU, Detailed Surface Scanner, SRV hangar and a light shield, and good heat behaviour for close fuel scooping. The consensus today places it just behind the Mandalay (more range, lighter) and the Anaconda (more range, far more module room) — but the Asp remains the sweet spot of cost, comfort and capability.

Where this hull shinesLong-range expeditions, exobiology tours, Detailed-Surface-Scanner mapping for credits, and road-to-riches first-discovery runs. Its canopy and heat profile make it especially pleasant for hands-on scooping and planetary approaches.

Four things make the Asp a genuine explorer rather than a multirole that tolerates exploration:

  • Class-5 FSD that engineers long. Increased Range + Mass Manager plus a Guardian FSD Booster puts laden jumps comfortably past 60 LY — few medium hulls reach further.
  • The canopy. The widest field of view in its class. It sounds cosmetic; on a thousand-jump expedition it's the difference between a chore and a joy.
  • Eight optional internals. Room for fuel scoop, AFMU, DSS, SRV bay, a light shield and spare module reinforcement — you never choose between survival kit and science kit.
  • Cool-running. Manageable heat for close fuel scooping and neutron-star supercharging, which keeps long hauls fast and safe.
The ceiling, stated honestlyThe Mandalay is lighter and jumps further for less mass; the Anaconda carries more and reaches further still; the Krait Phantom matches the Asp's range with a sleeker profile. The Asp is beaten on the single stat of maximum range — but it gives up almost nothing in practical expedition capability, and it's cheaper and comfier than all three.

The Asp Explorer is a credits-only purchase at most High-Tech and Industrial shipyards — no permit, rank or engineer gate, and no expansion requirement (it predates Odyssey). At roughly 6.1M Cr for the hull it sits in the affordable-but-meaningful bracket: cheap enough to be a sensible first 'real' explorer, expensive enough that you'll want to outfit it properly.

Use Inara's nearest-shipyard search to find stock near your base. The exploration fit — A-rated FSD, Guardian booster, fuel scoop, scanners — is where the real money goes (section 08).

Budget realityHull ~6M; a full A-rated exploration fit adds roughly 10–20M Cr depending on FSD grade and whether you fit the Guardian FSD Booster (unlocked, not bought). Engineering on top costs materials, not credits.

07

Initial Loadout — Buy-Only Plan

Goal: get into deep space cheaply but safely. Fit a 5A SCO Frame Shift Drive first — range is the whole point, and the SCO drive speeds in-system travel. Add a class-5 fuel scoop so you never strand, a Detailed Surface Scanner for mapping income, and an SRV hangar for planetary exobiology.

Strip weight everywhere else: D-rated power plant, thrusters, sensors and life support keep mass down and range up. A light shield generator and one heat sink cover the two ways explorers die — a botched planetary landing and an overheated fuel scoop. This buy-only starter runs roughly 8–12M Cr on top of the hull and is genuinely expedition-ready.

08

A-Rated Loadout — Upgrade Plan

To reach the best buy-only range and safety before engineering, upgrade in this order:

  • 1FSD to 5A (SCO). The largest single range jump available without engineering — do this first, always.
  • 2Guardian FSD Booster (class 3+). A flat range bonus on top of the FSD; unlock it at a Guardian structure rather than buying it.
  • 3Fuel Scoop to 6A. Faster scooping means less time sitting in a star's corona and more time covering distance.
  • 4Lightweight everything non-essential. D-rated cores and a small shield keep optimal mass low, which compounds with the FSD for extra range.
  • 5Second AFMU + DSS. Redundant repairs for long hauls and the scanner that turns every mapped body into credits.
Total A-rated costRoughly 12–20M Cr all-in depending on FSD and scoop grade. The Guardian FSD Booster is the highest-impact item and costs materials, not credits — unlock it before you leave the bubble.

Key Activities & Where To Do Them

Getting started
  • Detailed-Surface-Scanner mapping in unexplored systems near the bubble — honk on arrival, map high-value bodies (water worlds, ELWs, ammonia worlds) and bank the data.
  • Exobiology on atmospheric planets with the SRV and on-foot sampler — the single highest credits-per-hour in exploration right now.
  • Where: head a few hundred light-years off the beaten track in any direction; the Pleiades, California Nebula or simply the systems just past the populated bubble are full of unmapped bodies.
Advanced
  • Long expeditions to the galactic core, distant nebulae or named landmarks (e.g. Sagittarius A*) — the Asp's comfort and range make multi-thousand-jump trips bearable.
  • Neutron-star highway supercharging to extend jump range dramatically on long hauls — plan routes in Spansh.
  • Road to Riches first-discovery + first-mapped circuits for tens of millions in a single session.
  • Where: use Spansh's road-to-riches and neutron plotters; the EDSM map to find unvisited systems; and exobiology hotspots in the galactic arms.
Comfort noteThe Asp's canopy and cool running make it the hull people actually enjoy living in for weeks of exploration. If you plan long trips, that comfort is a real, if unquantifiable, advantage over a slightly longer-jumping but more cramped rival.
12

Field Notes — What Else To Know

  • Unlock the Guardian FSD Booster before you leave. It's the biggest range gain on the ship and it costs materials, not credits — don't depart the bubble without it.
  • Carry two AFMUs. One repairs the other; module wear on long hauls is real, and a dead FSD a thousand jumps out is a long self-destruct home.
  • Honk, then map selectively. Full system scans on arrival, then DSS only the high-value bodies — map efficiency is your credits-per-hour.
  • It can fight in a pinch. The two medium hardpoints mean you're not defenceless near the bubble, but exploration fits run them empty for weight — see the Asp Explorer Multipurpose dossier for a flex build.
  • Watch heat on the scoop. The Asp is cool, but a steep scoop on a hot star still bites — keep a heat sink mapped to a fire group.
VerdictThe Asp Explorer is the explorer the community recommends when someone asks for their first real deep-space ship, and it's still a top pick years later. 86/100 — out-jumped now by the Mandalay, Anaconda and Krait Phantom, but unmatched on the blend of range, that glorious canopy, full science-and-survival module room, and a price that doesn't sting. Fit the FSD and Guardian booster, point it at the black, and go.

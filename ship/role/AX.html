01

The Anti-Xeno Role

Anti-Xeno combat is the war against the Thargoids — a wholly separate discipline from human combat, with its own weapons, defences and tactics. Human guns barely scratch Thargoid armour; AX demands specialist hardware and a hull built to carry and survive it.

  • Interceptor hunting. Killing the large Thargoid Interceptors (Cyclops up to Hydra) — the headline AX activity, demanding Gauss, heart-sniping and constant evasion.
  • Scout swarms. Culling waves of small Thargoid Scouts — volume work that rewards many hardpoints and a big tank (the Type-10’s speciality).
  • AX Conflict Zones. Pitched battles against mixed Thargoid forces — tank and sustained firepower win here.
  • The Titan war. Strikes against the Maelstroms and Titans — long-range, heat-managed runs into the most hostile space in the game.
What makes an AX shipMedium hardpoints for Gauss Cannons (the AX workhorse), then brutal heat management (Gauss cook you alive), caustic resistance and a deep hull + Guardian Module/Hull Reinforcement stack to survive Thargoid lightning and caustic clouds. Agility to dodge attacks, plus support modules — Caustic Sink Launchers, an AFMU, a Xeno Scanner, a Shutdown Field Neutraliser — round it out. Shields are near-useless against Thargoids; AX is a hull-tank discipline.
02

How These Ships Are Scored

The 1–100 anti-Xeno rating weighs, in order:

  • Hardpoints for Gauss — especially medium mounts; AX firepower comes from a full Gauss spread with good convergence.
  • Heat capacity & management — Gauss Cannons generate enormous heat; a hull that runs cool fires more before overheating.
  • Hull tank & reinforcement room — internals for Guardian Hull/Module Reinforcement; shields barely matter against Thargoids.
  • Agility — dodging Thargoid lightning and interceptor attacks; the single biggest survival skill against interceptors.
  • Caustic handling & support slots — room for Caustic Sinks, an AFMU, a Xeno Scanner and a Shutdown Field Neutraliser.

The scale is roster-relative within the AX discipline. The top is a genuine tie between two philosophies — the agile medium Gauss platform (Krait Mk II) and the immovable tank (Cutter) — because both work, in different hands and different fights. Compare within a class first: mediums for interceptor duels, larges for conflict zones and the Titan war.

The three cost figures

  • Hull — the shipyard price of the bare ship.
  • A-rated fit (no engineering) — an approximate all-in credit total for a full role-appropriate loadout using the best buyable modules. A ballpark; your module choices swing it.
  • To engineer — engineering costs materials and engineer unlocks, not credits. A fully-engineered ship costs the same credits as its A-rated fit; the extra cost is a materials grind whose tier (Moderate / Heavy / Very heavy) scales with hull size.
The rebuy columnThe cost tables also list an approximate rebuy (~5% of insured value) — what you pay each time the ship is destroyed. It is the number that really governs how boldly you can fly.

Small-Pad AX

There is no small-pad AX ship. A Thargoid-ready loadout — multiple Gauss Cannons, a deep Guardian reinforcement stack, Caustic Sinks, an AFMU and a Xeno Scanner — simply will not fit on a small hull, and a small ship lacks the heat capacity and hull to survive a Thargoid encounter. AX begins at medium.

Why AX is a heavy grindOn top of normal engineering you need Guardian tech-broker unlocks (Gauss Cannons, Guardian Hull/Module Reinforcement, the FSD Booster) — each requiring Guardian site farming for components. Engineering still costs materials, not credits, but the AX materials tier is Heavy on the mediums and Very heavy on the larges, and the Guardian grind is the real time-sink. AX weapons themselves are not engineered — the work goes into heat, hull and reinforcement.

Two practical notes

  • Don’t bother with shields. Thargoid weapons bypass or shred shields; AX is a hull-tank discipline. Spend those slots on Guardian Hull/Module Reinforcement instead.
  • Bring the support kit. Caustic Sink Launchers (clear caustic damage), an AFMU (self-repair), a Xeno Scanner (find the hearts) and a Shutdown Field Neutraliser (survive the EMP) are not optional against interceptors.
09

Field Notes & Verdict

  • AX is a hull-tank game. Forget shields — survival is hull, Guardian reinforcement, caustic management and, above all, not getting hit. Agility saves more ships than armour.
  • Heat is the real limiter. Gauss Cannons cook you; a cool-running, Thermal-Spread power plant decides how many you can fire before you have to disengage.
  • Match the hull to the fight. Agile mediums for one-on-one interceptors; heavy larges for scout swarms, conflict zones and the Titan war. They are not interchangeable.
  • The Guardian grind gates everything. No Guardian unlocks, no AX — budget the site farming before the ship, because Gauss and reinforcement are what make any of these hulls work.
  • Start with the Chieftain. It teaches the evasion that keeps you alive in everything bigger — the consensus first AX ship for good reason.
VerdictFor interceptor hunting, the Krait Mk II (90) is the meta medium and the Alliance Chieftain (89) the agile teacher to start in. For the Titan war and conflict zones, the Imperial Cutter (90) tanks anything and the Type-10 Defender (86) owns scout swarms. Pick by the fight: agile Gauss platform, or immovable wall — both reach the top of this field.
